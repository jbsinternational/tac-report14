Goals

  • Increase access to SBIRT for adults in primary care and community health settings.
  • Ensure that SBIRT is used as the standard of care in South Carolina’s health care settings through State-level system and policy change.
  • Improve health and behavioral outcomes among adults with substance use disorders or substance use and co-occurring mental illness.

Implementation Sites

  • Eleven federally qualified health centers (FQHCs)
  • One private family practice
  • One internal medicine clinic

Population Served

  • Of South Carolina’s 46 counties, 25–31 are considered rural (55–67 percent of all counties). An estimated 21–25 percent of the State’s population is rural.
  • The population of focus is adult residents in six counties located in diverse areas of the State.
  • Patients are primarily low income and uninsured; many have unidentified alcohol, tobacco, and other drug issues; and an estimated 25 percent have an addiction.
