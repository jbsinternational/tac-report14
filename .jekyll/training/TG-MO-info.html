Transforming the Academic Preparation of Health Professionals: Competency-Based SBIRT Training
University of Missouri-Kansas City

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
Kansas City, Missouri

TI Number:
TI025355

Funding Period:
09/01/13–08/31/16

Grantee Project Director:
Heather Gotham, Ph.D.
Tel: 816-235-5058
Email: gothamhj@umkc.edu

Government Project Officer:
Erich Kleinschmidt, M.S.W., LICSW

Partnering Organizations

  • University of Missouri Kansas City (UMKC) School of Nursing and Health Studies (SoNHS)
  • UMKC School of Social Work (SSW)
  • UMKC School of Education (counseling and guidance)
  • UMKC School of Medicine residency programs
  • UMUK School of Dentistry
  • UMUK School of Pharmacy
  • UMUK SoNHS Collaborative for Excellence in Behavioral Health Research and Practice

Goals

  • Integrate SBIRT into the didactics and clinical practice experiences of UMKC’s baccalaureate nursing (BSN), nurse practitioner, and master of social work (MSW) programs.
  • Train approximately 300 BSN, 330 nurse practitioners, and 130 MSW health care providers to competently deliver SBIRT, as measured by an SBIRT adherence scale.
  • Develop and implement sustainable strategies to ensure the successful continuation of the project after the funding period ends and to disseminate SBIRT training to other local and statewide medical communities.

Implementation Sites

  • UMKC SoNHS
  • UMCK SSW
  • Field practicum experience in areas of community practice, including mental health (outpatient and inpatient services for children and adults), substance abuse, public and private schools, foster care/adoption, medical social work, aging services, homeless services, policy development, advocacy, hospice, university student services, domestic violence services, medical services for children and adults, community development, social work education, Veterans Administration facilities, and active military bases

Population Served

  • The Kansas City metro area has a population of 2.1 million and encompasses 15 counties in Missouri and Kansas. It is home to 5 teaching hospitals and 24 major hospital centers, including some that serve children or veterans.
  • Kansas City health care providers serve a large safety net population (uninsured or on Medicaid) of nearly 450,000 people—almost 25 percent of the entire population—through 17 safety net organizations operating 33 clinics.

Trainee Populations

Trainees include UMKC baccalaureate and advanced practice nursing students in four doctor of nurse practitioner (DNP) tracks, one master of science in nursing (MSN) track, and an SSW graduate track:

  • Adult-gerontology primary care nurse practitioner, SoNHS
  • Family nurse practitioner, SoNHS
  • Women’s health nurse practitioner, SoNHS
  • Pediatric nurse practitioner, SoNHS
  • Psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner, SoNHS
  • MSW students, SSW

There are a total of 228 students in year 1, 268 in year 2, and 268 in year 3, broken down as follows:

  • Approximately 44 faculty
  • Approximately 180 clinical preceptors
  • Approximately 300 BSNs
  • Approximately 330 nurse practitioners
  • Approximately 130 MSWs
  • Approximately 600 trainees in local/regional systems of care
  • Approximately 475 online continuing education unit (CEU) trainees
