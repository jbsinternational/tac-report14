SBIRT Training for Substance Misuse Program at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee
University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee

  • Site Visit Report

Goals

  • Teach all masters of social work (MSW) students the SBIRT module, and teach a subset of interested MSW students the SBIRT course material for State certification.
  • Train licensed professionals, particularly social work field supervisors, in SBIRT to support student practice in the field and create a workforce that can deliver reimbursable SBIRT services.
  • Promote SBIRT implementation, ongoing practice, and student training among participating community health centers serving the medically vulnerable

Implementation Sites

Implementation is occurring in three areas of the UWM social work curriculum:

  • MSW courses
  • MSW continuing education program for licensed professionals
  • Masters-level field placements, including clinical practice within multidisciplinary teams at community health centers serving the medically vulnerable

Population Served

  • Students are trained in community health centers serving the medically vulnerable, typically persons of color, in urban Milwaukee (an area known for high rates of substance misuse).
  • Approximately two-thirds of MSW graduates work in the greater Milwaukee area, primarily in urban settings serving the medically vulnerable and underserved.
  • Graduates who work in the health care industry typically work in publicly funded or religious medical systems that target vulnerable and underserved populations.
  • The Milwaukee area is a Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA)-designated medically underserved area, known in particular for urban poverty and infant mortality.
  • Wisconsin has one of the highest rates of past-month alcohol use, past-month binge alcohol use, and least perception of harm associated with weekly heavy drinking.

TRAINEE POPULATIONS

  • Approximately 320 MSW students
  • Approximately 150 students for reimbursable State certification (45 social workers, 30 nurse practitioners, 75 medical residents)
  • Approximately 125 CEUs for licensed clinical social workers, certified social workers, nurse practitioners, and physicians
