SBIRT Training for Substance Misuse Program at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee
University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Dates:
April 21–22, 2014

Site Visit Team Members:
Consulting Staff: Jennifer Kasten, Ph.D.; Denise Stockton, M.A.
Peer Consultant: Michael V. Pantalon, Ph.D.

Sites Visited:
Helen Bader School of Social Welfare

PROGRAM VISION AND DESIGN

Strengths

  • The University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee (UWM) SBIRT program seeks to increase future SBIRT medical system capacity by growing the number of students and licensed professionals outside the specialty alcohol and other drug abuse treatment system who have been introduced to or trained in SBIRT.
  • The UWM SBIRT program seeks to license professionals in placement settings to create a pipeline for graduates who work in these settings to bill for SBIRT services.

Challenges

  • Note noted

GRANTEE LEADERSHIP

Strengths

  • The council of directors meets to decide on staff hiring, review applications from M.S.W. students interested in the SBIRT training for State certification, assist with community partners, monitor M.S.W. students in implementing the SBIRT field component, and discuss and resolve program challenges.
  • The council of directors has developed recruitment and marketing materials for the SBIRT training program.

Challenges

  • Note noted

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Strengths

  • The SBIRT program is within the M.S.W. program for certification (Certified Alcohol and Drug Counselor).
  • The trauma component and trauma certificate program represent added strengths of the program.
  • Use of the “flipped classroom” model enhances learning.
  • SBIRT training is tailored for behavioral health, child and family, and gerontology specialty areas through role-plays.
  • The SBIRT Plus program training is a strong enhancement to the program.

Challenges

  • It will be a challenge to have a train-the-trainer approach as a sustainable model within partner agencies.

COMMUNITY LINKAGES, PARTNERS, AND PARTICIPATION

Strengths

  • The UWM Department of Social Work has a strong working relationship with the UWM College of Nursing and with medical clinics and providers in the greater Milwaukee area.
  • Final support provided to each partnering site will be through a dedicated person to implement SBIRT, supervise students, and assist with the evaluation.
  • The annual dinner for community partners helps strengthen relationships.

Challenges

  • None noted.

LEARNER RECRUITMENT

Strengths

  • Materials have been developed for the M.S.W. SBIRT training and certification program and the continuing education program.
  • Recruitment materials have been distributed.

Challenges

  • Note noted

AFFORDABLE CARE ACT READINESS

Strengths

  • SBIRT services delivered by licensed professionals, including ancillary staff, are billable under Medicaid and private insurers if supervised by an SBIRT-trained licensed physician or other similarly trained, licensed health care provider.
  • The UWM SBIRT program seeks to license professionals in placement settings to create a ready pipeline for graduates who work in these settings to bill for SBIRT services.

Challenges

  • Note noted

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Strengths

  • A train-the-trainer model will be implemented for Licensed Clinical Social Worker supervisors at participating federally qualified health centers so they can train other professionals in the SBIRT model on site.
  • SBIRT will become part of the required content in the master’s syllabus for Social Work 811: Direct Social Work Practice II.
  • The SBIRT intensive training and SBIRT Plus training build capacity for certified SBIRT professionals who can bill Medicaid and private payers for services.

Challenges

  • None noted.

GRANTEE EVALUATION

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

  • A comprehensive, multifaceted evaluation plan has been developed.
  • All process, outcome, and Government Performance and Results Act data had been collected and entered for all trainings through April 21, 2014. Baseline data collection exceeded 90 percent, and data collection for followup was up to 65 percent.

Challenges

  • Participant burden related to overall training time and the number of assessments are concerns.

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Grantee Leadership
    • Begin to shift the role of the council of directors toward program sustainability.
    • Consider having members of the council of directors serve as champions.
  • Implementation Plan
    • Develop reliably good trainers. Have trainees shadow the trainer and increase the responsibility for delivering training incrementally.
    • Provide training for conducting the brief negotiated interview.
    • Advanced-practice instructors should demonstrate their ability to perform SBIRT, particularly the steps of the brief negotiated interview.
    • Consider rolling out the protocol through the school system, learning community, and online. Have trainees sign to indicate they have read and understand the changes.
    • Use existing videos to train medical residents and professional students.
    • Contact Sharon Levy at Boston Children’s Hospital for information on the SBIRT protocol for adolescents.
    • Develop a coding scheme for role-play observation.
    • Give additional time to practice.
    • Give sufficient time to the trauma component.
  • Learner Recruitment
    • Gather anecdotal information on the program from students as champions to help tell the story for marketing purposes.
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Promote students as champions to tell the story for marketing purposes.
    • Use the existing train-the-trainer model.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Build in focus groups.
    • Consider reducing the number of assessments.
