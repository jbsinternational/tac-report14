University of Alaska Anchorage SBIRT
University of Alaska, Anchorage

  • Site Visit Report

Erich Kleinschmidt, M.S.W., LICSW

Partnering Organizations

  • University of Alaska, Anchorage (UAA)
  • Center for Behavioral Health Research and Services
  • Alaska Family Medicine Residency program
  • Interprofessional Health Science Simulation Center

Goals

  • Adapt SBIRT curricula to meet Alaska’s goal.
  • Increase collaboration among campus partners.
  • Establish curricula that support diversity.
  • Disseminate SBIRT training throughout Alaska.
  • Maximize medical system capacity to address substance abuse.
  • Assure sustainability.

Implementation Sites

  • UAA’s College of Health
    • School of Nursing
    • School of Social Work
  • College of Arts and Sciences
    • UAA Psychology Department, University of Alaska, Fairbanks
    • UAA Psychological Services Center
  • Alaska Family Medicine Residency program
  • Interprofessional Health Science Simulation Center
  • Arctic-SBIRT Council of Directors
  • Residential and inpatient psychiatric and substance abuse treatment programs
  • Community-based clinical settings, including public health clinics, schools, home health, outpatient clinics, emergency departments, and others
  • Social work field practica in settings such as mental health agencies, schools, hospitals, nonprofit social service agencies, residential treatment facilities, and others

Population Served

  • Substance abuse, including alcohol, tobacco, and illegal or prescription drugs used for nonmedical reasons, is the cause or contributing factor in 9 out of the 10 top causes of death in Alaska (State of Alaska epidemiologic study).
  • More than 10 percent of all hospitalized injury patients in Alaska had suspected or proven drug use.
  • Substance abuse disproportionately affects Alaska Natives, who make up approximately 15 percent of Alaska’s population.
  • UAA graduates work in remote Alaskan communities that experience disparities in access to care and a disproportionate burden of substance misuse.
  • Alaska family medicine residents are trained to practice with medically underserved populations in challenging primary care settings such as community health centers, urban underserved practices, rural practices, and tribal health systems.

Trainee Populations

Approximately 932 students are in the following disciplines:

  • Medical residency (family medicine)
  • Nursing
  • Social work
  • Psychology
