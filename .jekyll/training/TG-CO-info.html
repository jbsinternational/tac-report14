University of Colorado Denver

  • Site Visit Report

Partnering Organizations

  • University of Colorado College of Nursing (CU CON)
  • SBIRT Colorado program
  • Denver Health
  • University of Colorado Health
  • Sheridan Health Services federally qualified health center (FQHC)
  • Children’s Hospital of Colorado
  • Denver Veterans Administration Hospital
  • A center for Native American health research
  • Peer Assistance Services, Inc.

Goals

  • Establish a structure that will provide undergraduate and graduate nursing students with essential SBIRT knowledge and skills.
  • Train undergraduate and graduate students at CU CON on essential knowledge, attitudes, and skills to deliver SBIRT in multiple settings, with a focus on multidisciplinary hospital-based or primary care, medically underserved, veterans, and other at-risk patients.
  • Integrate SBIRT skills into nursing students’ supervised patient care experiences in a variety of multidisciplinary clinical practice settings.
  • Develop a plan for sustainable SBIRT training and SBIRT skills.

Implementation Sites

  • CON programs for nurse practitioners, clinical nurse midwives, and clinical nurse specialists
  • Clinical practice at partner organization sites

Population Served

  • CU CON trains students to work with low-income patients, minority groups, rural residents, medically underserved populations, veterans, and other at-risk groups.
  • After graduation most students go on to work with these vulnerable groups. Many graduates work in practice settings across Colorado, with 9.2 percent serving patients in rural areas. Also, 4.1 percent serve patients in nearby States such as Arizona, New Mexico, Nebraska, Wyoming, Oklahoma, Utah, and Montana.

TRAINEE POPULATIONS

  • Approximately 675 undergraduate nursing students
  • Approximately 150 advanced practice nursing graduate students
  • Approximately 45 faculty
