Enhancing SBIRT Utilization Among Underserved Populations in the Bronx, New York, and Surrounding Urban Areas: A Lehman College Training Initiative for Social Work Students and Medical Residents
City University of New York, Lehman College

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
Bronx, New York

TI Number:
TI025382

Funding Period:
09/01/13–08/31/16

Grantee Project Director:
Evan Senreich, Ph.D., M.S.W.
Tel: 212-642-8594
Email: evan.senreich@lehman.cuny.edu

Government Project Officer:
Kellie Cosby, M.P.H.

Partnering Organizations

  • Bronx-Lebanon Hospital Center
  • City University of New York/Lehman College

Goals

  • Provide SBIRT training to at least 50 second-year master of social work (MSW) students and at least 100 senior-year bachelor of social work (BSW) students at Lehman College each year.
  • For each of the 3 years, ensure that 80 percent of the 150 social work student trainees use SBIRT in their field placements at health care and social service agencies in the Bronx and surrounding urban areas.
  • Ensure that 80 percent of MSW and BSW social work program graduates who were trained in SBIRT will use SBIRT in settings where they are employed or interning 1 year after completing the SBIRT training.
  • Provide effective SBIRT training to all 42 medical residents at Bronx-Lebanon Ambulatory Care Pediatric Clinic in the first year of the project.
  • Provide SBIRT training to 15 additional medical residents in the second and third years of the project.
  • Ensure that 80 percent of the medical residents at Bronx-Lebanon Ambulatory Care Pediatric Clinic use SBIRT with their patients 1 year after their training.

Implementation Sites

  • Bronx-Lebanon Hospital, Pediatric Ambulatory Care Clinic
  • Lehman College
  • Health care and social service settings in impoverished, underserved areas of the Bronx and surrounding urban communities

Population Served

  • The Bronx, with a population of 1.4 million, is the poorest county in New York State, with a poverty rate of 28.5 percent (United States Census Bureau, 2013).
  • The Bronx’s rate of hospitalization for drug problems from 2008 through 2010 was 68.1 per 10,000—well over twice New York State’s rate and the highest of any county in the State (New York State Department of Health, 2013).
  • In 2010 the population of the Bronx was 54 percent Hispanic and 30 percent Black, according to the New York City Department of Planning, and 56 percent of the population reported a language other than English spoken at home.
  • The Bronx has a higher rate of hospitalizations for drug problems than any county in New York State (New York State Department of Health, 2013).  

Trainee Populations

  • Approximately 150 MSW students
  • Approximately 300 BSW students
  • Approximately 72 medical residents
