Launching SBIRT Into the Community: A Multidisciplinary Approach
The Catholic University of America

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
Washington, District of Columbia

TI Number:
TI025411

Funding Period:
09/01/13–08/31/16

Grantee Project Director:
Karlynn BrintzenhofeSzoc, Ph.D., LCSW-C
Tel: 202-319-5583
Email: brintzek@cua.edu

Government Project Officer:
Kellie Cosby, M.P.H.

Partnering Organizations

  • The Catholic University of America (CUA), National Catholic School of Social Services (NCSSS)
  • CUA School of Nursing

Goals

  • Develop and integrate SBIRT training into the educational curriculum of all master of social work (MSW) and master of science in nursing (MSN) students.
  • Train faculty in both schools in all aspects of SBIRT and identify and train teams of faculty to incorporate the model in courses across both curricula.
  • Expand and prepare the workforce by training field instructors, clinical preceptors, community providers, and administrators in SBIRT, including support in incorporating SBIRT into the standard of care.
  • Integrate student SBIRT knowledge, skills, and attitudes to enhance their ability to effect positive behavior change in their clients.
  • Sustain the training program and community outreach beyond the grant period through permanent curriculum changes, continuous training of field instructors and clinical preceptors, and ongoing SBIRT training for community providers and administrators.

Implementation Sites

  • CUA NCSSS
  • CUA School of Nursing
  • NCSSS and School of Nursing field placements and clinical practica in community health centers, hospitals, community service providers, charitable organizations, wellness centers, school-based health clinics in high-immigrant communities, a clinic for the homeless population, county health departments, and military and veterans facilities

Population Served

  • More than half of clinical settings where MSN students provide clinical care are in health and mental health manpower shortage areas or federally qualified clinics and 98 percent of NCSSS placements serve vulnerable and underserved populations.
  • Upon graduating, students will work with clients of diverse racial and ethnic backgrounds, vulnerable and at-risk clients, and active duty military and veterans and their families.

Trainee Populations

An approximate total of 450 trainees (150 per year) include the following:

  • MSW and MSN graduate students
  • NCSSS and School of Nursing faculty members
  • Nursing and social work professionals who provide clinical supervision to students and other professional nurses and social workers
