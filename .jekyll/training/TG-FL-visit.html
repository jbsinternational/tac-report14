Integrating SBIRT Into Primary Care: A School of Nursing Training Program
University of Miami

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Dates:
May 6–7, 2014

Site Visit Team Members:
Consulting Staff: Amanda Gmyrek, Ph.D.; Denise Stockton, M.A.
Peer Consultant: Sally Tirado, M.A., LCSW

Sites Visited:
ConnectFamilias
University of Miami Hospital
St. John Bosco Free Clinic

PROGRAM VISION AND DESIGN

Strengths

  • The University of Miami (UM) SBIRT program has shown sensitivity to cross-cultural and translation issues with community and partner input regarding approaches and materials.
  • The program preplanned for potential pitfalls and challenges and to achieve the greatest training efficiency and effectiveness.

Challenges

  • None noted

GRANTEE LEADERSHIP

Strengths

  • The program’s organizational structure is functionally designed to attain the targeted goals.
  • The council of directors is composed of representatives from each of the participating partners who are invested in the project’s success.

Challenges

  • None noted.

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Strength

  • The program has thoughtfully planned for potential pitfalls and challenges and to achieve the greatest training efficiency and effectiveness.
  • The program has integrated SBIRT into the electronic medical records at the University of Miami Hospital (UMH).
  • The program has strong partnerships within UM, UMH, Florida International University, and community-based organizations.
  • The program’s leadership includes Doctors of Nursing Practice with applied links to SBIRT implementation.

Challenges

  • The program includes limited opportunities for direct observation and feedback for trainees.

COMMUNITY LINKAGES, PARTNERS, AND PARTICIPATION

Strengths

  • The program has formed strong community partnerships with diverse organizations (i.e., free clinic, university hospital, community outreach).
  • The program shows sensitivity to cross-cultural and translation issues with community and partner input regarding approaches and materials.
  • The program has built strong relationships with behavioral health referral networks.
  • The program’s statewide network will further SBIRT implementation.

Challenges

  • None noted.

LEARNER RECRUITMENT

Strength

  • The team has successfully recruited a diverse set of health care students and professionals for SBIRT and motivational interviewing training.

Challenges

  • None noted.

AFFORDABLE CARE ACT READINESS

Strength

  • SBIRT has been integrated into the electronic medical record at UMH.

Challenges

  • Although SBIRT is being integrated into the electronic medical record, there are currently two different systems that do not interface.

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Strength

  • The team has also identified and engaged strong champions at the participating professional schools and partnering organizations.
  • A train-the-trainer model is being used so that training will be self-perpetuating.

Challenges

  • Reimbursement for SBIRT services is a challenge reported for both the hospital and community-based partners. The grantee reported that reimbursement is a key to sustainability, and the council of directors indicated the project team has different levels of need related to reimbursement.

GRANTEE EVALUATION

Strength

  • The program’s approach to evaluation has been one of continuous quality improvement.
  • A comprehensive evaluation plan has been developed.
  • Process and outcome data will be collected.

Challenges

  • There is no Spanish translation for the Government Performance and Results Act (GPRA) satisfaction survey, posing a challenge for the grantee in reaching community workers in Hispanic neighborhoods who have direct access to hard-to-reach families but have poor English-speaking skills.
  • The response rate for the GPRA 30-day followup is low.
  • Qualitative versus quantitative training: It is important to understand how to ensure indepth understanding of SBIRT skills so SBIRT is integrated in a meaningful way.

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Implementation Plan
    • Increase opportunities for practice and feedback for trainers receiving intensive motivational interviewing training.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Time the initial training so that students are still in class for the GPRA 30-day followup.

Potential Technical Assistance Requests

  • Sustainability Planning
    • Identify ways to secure reimbursement for SBIRT services.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Obtain information about how to translate the GPRA satisfaction survey into Spanish.
