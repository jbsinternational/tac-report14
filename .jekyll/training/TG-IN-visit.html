Advancing Multidisciplinary Education for SBIRT
Indiana University

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Dates:
April 8–9, 2014

Site Visit Team Members:
Consulting Staff: Marcus Hudson, M.S.; Kevin Hylton, Ph.D.
Peer Consultant: Janice Pringle, Ph.D.

Sites Visited:
Eskenazi Health
Indiana University School of Nursing
Indiana University School of Medicine

PROGRAM VISION AND DESIGN

Strengths

  • Indiana University’s SBIRT program is using the SAMHSA core curriculum to tailor the Web-based curriculum proposed.
  • The program will use interactive Web-based training.

Challenges

  • None noted

GRANTEE LEADERSHIP

Strengths

  • The program involves staff from previous and currently funded SAMHSA SBIRT grants.
  • The program is composed of staff from each targeted discipline (medicine, nursing, and social work).
  • The program has members from the Indiana State policy steering committee involved with the grant.

Challenges

  • The program has not formed a functional council of directors.

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Strengths

  • Training sites are varied throughout the State.
  • Members of the project staff have existing, longstanding relationships with community preceptors.

Challenges

  • Preceptors have not been trained.

COMMUNITY LINKAGES, PARTNERS, AND PARTICIPATION

Strengths

  • The program is collaborating with two highly experienced community partners—Indiana Prevention Resource Center and Midtown Community Mental Health Center—to reach professionals throughout the State’s health care delivery system.

Challenges

  • None noted.

CLIENT OUTREACH, RECRUITMENT, AND REFERRAL

Strengths

  • The program will make Web-based modules, training manuals, and teaching materials available outside the program to expand SBIRT outreach.
  • Teleconferences will be used to facilitate training throughout the State

Challenges

  • None noted.

AFFORDABLE CARE ACT READINESS

Strengths

  • None noted.

Challenges

  • None noted.

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Strengths

  • Buy-in and commitment have been obtained from the professors and clinical faculty across all levels in the various disciplines.
  • The program has the full support of the deans and the directors of the specialty areas in all three schools.

Challenge

  • The program has not formed a functional council of directors.

GRANTEE EVALUATION

Strengths

  • Indiana University’s SBIRT program has experienced evaluators from previously funded SBIRT programs.
  • The program has a well-developed evaluation plan.
  • The evaluation team will use several evaluation tools to assess the overall effectiveness and quality of the training, including the Car, Relax, Alone, Forget, Friends, Trouble screen (known as CRAFFT), the Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test, the Baseline Training Satisfaction Tool, and the Followup Training Satisfaction Tool.

Challenges

  • None noted.

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Grantee Leadership
    • Consider using members of the Indiana State Policy Steering committee to form the council of directors and develop a strategic sustainability plan.
  • Implementation Plan
    • Consider training the preceptors to increase buy-in among the training sites.
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Form a council of directors to focus on a strategic plan for sustainability.

Potential Technical Assistance Requests

  • Grantee Leadership
    • Consider using members of the Indiana State policy steering committee to form the council of directors and develop a strategic sustainability plan.
  • Implementation Plan
    • Consider training the preceptors to increase buy-in among the training sites.
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Form a council of directors to focus on a strategic plan for sustainability.
