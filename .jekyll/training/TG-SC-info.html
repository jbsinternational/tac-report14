InterDisciplinary Collaboration and Implementation of SBIRT Curricula in University of South Carolina
University of South Carolina

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
Columbia, South Carolina

TI Number:
TI025374

Funding Period:
09/01/13–08/31/16

Grantee Project Director:
Shilpa Srinivasan, M.D.
Tel: 803-434-4019
Email: shilpa.srinivasan@uscmed.sc.edu  

Government Project Officer:
Kellie Cosby, M.P.H.

Partnering Organizations

  • University of South Carolina
  • South Carolina Department of Mental Health
  • South Carolina HIV/AIDS Clinical Training Center

Goals

  • Educate health professionals in didactic and practice settings to implement and promote the use of SBIRT as a strategy for reaching more individuals in South Carolina at risk for substance use disorders (SUDs).
  • Develop SBIRT training modules that will enhance the knowledge and skills of newly trained professionals to identify persons with SUDs, use motivational interviewing techniques to conduct brief intervention, and provide referral to ongoing treatment as needed.
  • Underscore the importance of providing SBIRT as part of an interdisciplinary treatment team approach as one component of promoting behavioral health across medical settings.
  • Disseminate SBIRT training into local and statewide medical communities that serve individuals with SUDs.

Implementation Sites

  • University of South Carolina departments
    • Rehabilitation Counseling Program (RCP)
    • Neuropsychiatry (NPSY) Residency Program
    • Family medicine and preventive medicine
    • Internal medicine (INTM)
    • College of Nursing
    • College of Social Work
  • Four primary care medical residency programs at Palmetto Health/University of South Carolina School of Medicine
  • Ryan White Statewide Clinics and associated clinics in South Carolina
  • South Carolina Department of Mental Health sites located across South Carolina
    • Hospitals (4)
    • Community mental health centers (17)
    • Clinics (50)
  • South Carolina HIV/AIDS Clinical Training Center
  • National Association of Social Workers

Population Served

  • The South Carolina population is geographically dispersed. Nearly 75 percent is designated as rural, and at least some portion of all but two of the State’s counties are medically underserved (SCORH, 2013).
  • Approximately 7 percent of South Carolina adult residents are estimated to be dependent on alcohol, 7 percent dependent on marijuana, and 12 percent dependent on cocaine (Kandel et al., 1997).
  • RCP graduates work with transition-age and adult persons with physical and/or psychiatric disabilities, person with serious and long-term mental illnesses and/or co-occurring disorders (mental illness and substance abuse), and students with learning disabilities, developmental disabilities, and/or other disabilities.
  • NPSY residents encounter substance use patients on the majority of their rotations. They serve persons with serious mental illness or mental conditions—who are 61 percent more likely to abuse alcohol, 115 percent more likely to abuse illicit drugs, and 287 percent more likely to be dependent on illicit drugs than are other persons (Brucker, 2007).
  • The resident ambulatory clinic population served by INTM residents is all adult, predominantly low income with only Medicaid insurance, and more than 50 percent black.

TRAINEE POPULATIONS

  • A total of 630 graduate and medical residency students (210 per year for 3 years)
  • A total of 3,500 physicians, nurses, and clinical social workers including the following:
    • Approximately 1,300 from the South Carolina Department of Mental Health
    • Approximately 2,000 from the South Carolina HIV/AIDS Clinical Training Center
    • Approximately 200 from National Association of Social Workers
