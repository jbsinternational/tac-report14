Pennsylvania SBIRT Interdisciplinary Training Program
Allegheny-Singer Research Institute

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

TI Number:
TI025406

Funding Period:
09/01/13–08/31/16

Grantee Project Director:
William Johnjulio, M.D.
Tel: 412-457-1050
Email: bjohnjul@wpahs.org

Government Project Officer:
Reed Forman, M.S.W.

Partnering Organizations

  • West Penn Allegheny Health System (WPAHS)
  • University of Pittsburg

Goals

  • Train medical residents, medical students, dental students, nursing students, and pharmacy students in SBIRT practices.
  • Disseminate SBIRT practices throughout WPAHS and other clinical practice sites.

Implementation Sites

  • WPAHS residencies
    • Family medicine
    • Emergency medicine
  • Lake Erie College of Osteopathic Medicine
    • Medical
    • Dental
    • Pharmacy
  • West Penn Hospital Nursing School
  • Practice sites
    • Emergency department (ED)
    • Community-based primary care site
    • Urgent care site
    • Long-term care facility
    • Community pharmacy
    • Clinics and federally qualified health care (FQHC) settings

Population Served

  • The patient population in Allegheny County—where most residents, medical students, and nursing students would engage in a clinical rotation—has a mean age of 41.3; is 81.5 percent White and 13.2 percent Black; and has a per capita income of $29,436.
  • The ED patients have a lower socioeconomic status and use the ED for primary care. The ED has a high prevalence of patients with substance use disorders, chronic and unstable illnesses, and serious and persistent mental illness.
  • The patients at community-based primary care sites reflect the demographics in the community where the care site is located, with one exception—persons aged 18–30 do not access primary care often, except for incidental acute illnesses.
  • Urgent care patients are often underinsured (in a lower, but not the lowest, socioeconomic strata), aged 18–30, and do not have a primary care physician; or they are older persons with chronic illnesses.
  • Long-term care facility patients are older persons with chronic illnesses, end-stage illnesses, and various disabilities.
  • Hospital care patients and hospital pharmacy patients are from all socioeconomic levels. They are often older and have chronic illnesses or are younger and have acute illnesses.
  • The community pharmacy has a bimodal population of children who need treatment for acute illnesses and older persons who need chronic disease medications.
  • Patients in clinics and FQHCs are often in lower socioeconomic strata and are underemployed, unemployed, homeless, or precariously housed. They have a higher prevalence of behavioral health problems.

Trainee Populations

  • Medical students
  • Nursing students
  • Dental students
  • Pharmacy students
  • Family medicine and emergency medicine residents
