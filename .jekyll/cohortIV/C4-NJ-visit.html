New Jersey

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Dates:
September 12–13, 2013

Site Visit Team Members:
Joseph Hyde, M.A., LMHC
Denise Stockton, M.A.
Angela McKinney Jones, M.S.S.W.
Steve O’Neil, M.A.
Kellie Cosby, M.P.H.

Sites Visited:
New Jersey Department of Mental Health and Addiction Services
Henry J. Austin Health Center, Inc. (HJAHC)
Chambers Manor Family Medical Practice (subsidiary of HJAHC)

Program Vision and Design

Strengths

  • Implementation of screening, brief intervention, and referral to treatment (SBIRT) is part of the strategic vision for the State.
  • The SBIRT initiative has senior-level administrative buy-in and support from the Department of Mental Health and Addiction Services (DMHAS).

Challenges

  • DMHAS is a newly formed State agency that resulted from a merger of substance abuse and mental health services. Significant turnover in staff leadership occurred as a result of the merger.
  • Although the strategic goal of Trenton Health Team (THT)—to become an accountable care organization with affiliated patient-centered medical homes—is aligned with the future of emerging health care delivery systems, it will require negotiation between THT and DMHAS to determine where SBIRT integrates into this emerging system.
  • THT should continue to make SBIRT a priority and allocate sufficient time and effort to meeting its deliverables.

Grantee Leadership

Strengths

  • The grantee has successfully implemented SBIRT in three primary care clinics.
  • The grantee implementation team worked with each site to tailor the implementation to the workflow and culture of each organization.
  • The grantee has employed a public health planning model using population data to target where SBIRT services will be implemented.
  • The grantee experienced startup delays and, as a result, is behind on its performance targets.

Challenges

  • The grantee experienced startup delays and, as a result, is behind on its performance targets.

Implementation

Strengths

  • Each practice site has developed a workflow tailored to its unique operations.
  • Practice sites are successfully serving diverse patient populations.
  • Each practice site uses a team approach in the delivering SBIRT services and using electronic technologies to support the efforts.
  • All practice sites have EHRs.
  • SBIRT data collection and reporting are managed within the DMHAS information technology (IT) department, using a sophisticated Web-based data system.

Challenges

  • In fall 2012, multiple events converged to create challenging conditions affecting startup and implementation.
  • The grantee has difficulty meeting the performance targets because of errors calculating the number of patients to be served by THT.
  • Staff at practice sites are still learning about SBIRT and motivational interviewing.
  • The State has limited capacity for training, practice site technical assistance, and workforce development.
  • There is no data interface between the State’s IT system and the practice sites’ EHRs; consequently, patient data require dual entry. IT staff are reluctant to allow such an interface because of concerns regarding practice site data quality.
  • No clearly defined set of procedures exists between the practice sites and the evaluation team for Government Performance and Results Act (GPRA) followup.
  • There is limited physician involvement in SBIRT at the practice sites.
  • The policy steering committee was not scheduled to hold its first full meeting until October 2013.

Affordable Care Act Readiness

Strength

  • THT is modifying its organizational structure to become an accountable care organization supported by a network of patient-centered medical homes.

Challenge

  • The roles of behavioral health care and SBIRT do not appear to be fully developed or understood within the THT model.

Sustainability Planning

Strengths

  • The New Jersey DMHAS has identified SBIRT as part of an overarching strategy.
  • SBIRT services are embedded within EHRs at practice sites.

Challenges

  • Medicaid does not reimburse for SBIRT services. This presents challenges to developing a viable business model for SBIRT in primary care settings.
  • Practice sites have not developed a business practice model that would support SBIRT beyond grant funding.

Grantee Evaluation

Strengths

  • The State has contracted with an evaluation and research group experienced in large-scale followup activities among populations that are sometimes difficult to reach.
  • Beyond GPRA followup, the grantee plans to conduct formative, outcome, and cost-benefit evaluation activities.

Challenges

  • The lack of interface between EHRs and the data collection system requires additional staff labor to complete dual data entries.
  • State and the evaluator have not fully developed a plan for 6-month GPRA followup.

Recommended Enhancements

Grantee-led Enhancements

  • Program Vision and Design
    • Maintain ongoing communication between DMHAS senior leadership and THT to ensure the appropriate role and priority of SBIRT in the emerging THT system.
  • Grantee Leadership
    • Complete the corrective action plan addressing strategies to achieve performance targets.
  • Implementation
    • Determine what additional practice site capacity is needed to meet performance targets.
    • Develop a comprehensive training and technical assistance plan to support grantee programs.
    • Explore the practice site data quality issues impeding the development of an electronic interface between the State and practice sites.
    • Clarify the relationship and reporting procedures between the practice sites and the evaluator regarding GPRA followup.
  • Affordable Care Act Readiness
    • Continue planning discussions addressing the role of SBIRT and overall behavioral health integration within the THT programs.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Prioritize completion of the GPRA followup plan and a plan to train and support data collection activities at the practice sites.

Potential Technical Assistance Requests

  • Implementation
    • Develop a comprehensive training and technical assistance plan to support grantee programs.
    • Consider seeking technical assistance to build strategies for developing physician SBIRT champions and for improving the integration of SBIRT within a primary care practice model.
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Consider seeking technical assistance in developing strategies to support SBIRT code activation within Medicaid and third-party payers.
    • Consider seeking technical assistance to support practice sites in the development of viable business practice models for SBIRT within primary care settings.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Prioritize completion of the GPRA followup plan and a plan to train and support data collection activities at the practice sites.
