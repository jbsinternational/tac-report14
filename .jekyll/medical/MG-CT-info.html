SBIRT Training in Yale Residency Programs
Yale University School of Medicine

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
New Haven, Connecticut

TI Number:
TI020253

Funding Period:
09/30/08–09/29/13

Grantee Project Director:
Gail D'Onofrio, M.D.
Tel: 203-785-4404
Email: gail.donofrio@yale.edu

Visit the Yale University School of Medicine SBIRT Website

Government Project Officer:
Kellie Cosby, M.P.H.

Partnering Organizations

  • Project ASSERT
  • APT Foundation
  • Southern Connecticut Rehabilitation Center
  • Multicultural Ambulatory Addiction Services

Goals

  • Expand and adapt the SBIRT curriculum for multiple practitioners and specialties.
  • Modify microsystems in practice sites to ensure long-term adoption of SBIRT.
  • Incorporate effective communication and cultural competency.
  • Develop enduring instructional strategies and disseminate teaching materials, tools, and other resources.

Implementation Sites

  • Yale New Haven Hospital (YNHH) Primary Care Center
    • Adolescent Clinic
    • Adult Clinic
  • YNHH Emergency Departments (EDs)
    • Pediatric ED
    • Adult ED (Bridgeport Hospital)
  • Yale Women’s Center
  • Yale Maternal-Fetal Medicine Clinic
  • Chase Outpatient Center of Waterbury Hospital
  • Family Health Center of St. Mary’s Hospital (Waterbury)
  • Yale High-Risk Obstetrical Clinic
  • Connecticut Mental Health Center
  • Yale Psychiatric Hospital

Population Served

  • According to 2006 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System data, Connecticut ranks in the top 20 percent for use of marijuana, cocaine, and nonmedical use of pain relievers for persons aged 18–25 ( 15.2 percent).
  • New Haven, the third largest city in Connecticut, has approximately 123,626 residents. It ranks among the poorest cities in the United States. Approximately 24 percent of residents live below the poverty level, compared with 7.9 percent of Connecticut residents and 12.4 percent in the Nation.
  • New Haven’s racial composition is 43.5 percent White, 37.4 percent Black, and 21.4 percent Hispanic or Latino (of any race).
  • The majority of YNHH patients are female, minority, and receiving care under entitlements (Medicare and Medicaid).

Trainee Populations

  • Residents in five primary care specialties
    • Internal medicine
    • Psychiatry
    • Emergency medicine
    • Obstetrics/gynecology
    • Pediatrics
  • Physician assistants, nurses, social workers, and other non physicians who participate on teams
