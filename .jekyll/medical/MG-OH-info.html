Goals

  • Develop and implement a level 1 training program at KMC that teaches residents how to provide SBIRT to patients.
  • Develop and implement a level 2 training program at KMC that teaches residents about SBIRT principles and how to screen for patients.
  • Create and deliver additional training programs that teach KMC nonresidents (students), medical professionals, and support staff about SBIRT.
  • Enhance patient care throughout KMC by providing SBIRT in a way that identifies and serves more patients.
  • Promote adoption of SBIRT by delivering training to local and statewide communities

Population Served

  • Patients come from Montgomery (which includes the city of Dayton), Greene, Warren, Miami, and Butler Counties in Ohio, where the population is 1.35 million, and 1 in 10 individuals are likely to have a substance abuse problem.
  • Dayton has a high level of drug activity, attributed largely to the city’s proximity to the interstate freeway system. Consequently, the prevalence of methamphetamine, benzodiazepine, and opioid-related substance abuse is particularly high among patients.
  • The patient population is 85 percent White and 15 percent minority (Black, Hispanic, Asian, or more than one race).
  • One in 10 patients live below the poverty level.
  • Approximately one-third of patients are younger than 18.
  • Approximately 15 percent of patients over age 5 have a disability.

Trainee Populations

  • Up to 63 KMC medical residents in year 1, from internal medicine, psychiatry, transitional year, and emergency medicine
  • Up to 160 KMC nonresidents annually, including nursing students, physician assistant students, and clinical pastoral education students
