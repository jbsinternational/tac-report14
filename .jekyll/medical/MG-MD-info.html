University of Maryland
MD3 (Maryland M.D.s Making a Difference)

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Partnering Organizations

  • University of Maryland, Baltimore
  • University of Maryland, Baltimore County
  • Baltimore City Health Department Preventing Substance Exposed Pregnancies initiative
  • Maryland Planned Parenthood
  • Johns Hopkins University Internal Medicine Residency Program
  • Sinai Hospital Internal Medicine Residency Program
  • State mental health and substance abuse administrations

Goals

  • Create an innovative and comprehensive medical residency training curriculum for SBIRT for individuals who misuse, abuse, or are dependent on substances including illegal drugs, prescription medications, alcohol, and nicotine.
  • Integrate training and procedures into six participating University of Maryland Medical Center (UMMC) primary care residency programs encompassing approximately 330 residents.
  • Train approximately 35 fellows in related fellowship programs and residents rotating through the Shock Trauma Center in the first phase of the program.

Implementation Sites

  • Six UMMC primary care residency programs
    • Emergency Medicine
    • Family Medicine
    • Internal Medicine
    • Obstetrics and Gynecology (OB/GYN)
    • Pediatrics
    • Psychiatry
  • Related fellowship programs (e.g., Child and Adolescent Psychiatry, Behavioral Pediatrics)
  • UMMC Shock Trauma Center
  • Clinics
    • UMMC Family Medicine Clinic
    • Psychiatry Emergency Services and Addiction Medicine Fellows on the UMMC Substance Abuse Consult Service
    • UMMC OB/GYN Resident Clinic
    • UMMC Emergency Department
    • Mercy Medical Center Emergency Department
    • Veterans Affairs Medical Center Emergency Department
    • University Health Center
    • Veterans Affairs Clinic
    • Faculty Practice Clinic
    • Mercy Hospital Clinic
    • UMMC Pediatric Emergency Department
    • PATH Outpatient Clinic
    • UMMC Inpatient Pediatric Services

 

Population Served

  • UMMC is located in west Baltimore. 
  • Baltimore’s population of approximately 631,000 residents is 64.8 percent African American, 31.7 percent Caucasian, 2.4 percent Hispanic, and 1.9 percent Asian. UMMC is the primary hospital for the largely low-income residents of west Baltimore. 
  • Because UMMC is a tertiary care hospital for various transplant services, oncology, and the Shock Trauma Center, it serves patients from across the State, the surrounding States (Delaware, Virginia, West Virginia, and Pennsylvania), and Washington, DC.

Trainee Populations

Over the 5-year period, MD3 anticipates training the following numbers of individuals:

  • Approximately 1,500 UMMC and other program residents (full curriculum)
  • Approximately 1,200 residents (abbreviated curriculum)
  • Approximately 520 practicing physicians and other health care professionals (regional and statewide trainings)
