SBIRT Medical and Residency Training Program (SMaRT)
University of Pittsburgh School of Pharmacy

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

TI Number:
TI020263

Grantee Project Director:
Tel: 412-904-6127
Email: jlp127@pitt.edu

Visit the Program Evaluation and Research Unit SBIRT Website

Government Project Officer:
Erich Kleinschmidt, M.S.W., LICSW

Partnering Organizations

  • University of Pittsburgh General Internal Medicine (GIM)
  • University of Pittsburgh Medical Center (UPMC): teaching hospitals in McKeesport, Shadyside, and St. Margaret
  • Susquehanna Health System, Regional Medical Center: Williamsport Hospital and Medical Center
  • Albert Einstein Medical Center (AEMC)
  • West Penn Allegheny Health System, Allegheny General Hospital
  • Walter Reed National Military Medical Center, Preventive Medicine Department and Readiness Clinic
  • MedRespond LLC
  • Pennsylvania Office of Medical Assistance Programs
  • Allegheny County Overdose Prevention Coalition

Goals

  • Train medical residents effectively in SBIRT practices.
  • Disseminate SBIRT practices throughout the medical community.

Implementation Sites

  • University of Pittsburgh GIM
  • UPMC-McKeesport: Family Medicine, Psychiatry, and Obstetrics/Gynecology (OB/GYN) Departments
  • UPMC-Shadyside: Family Medicine Department
  • UPMC-St. Margaret: Family Medicine Department
  • Susquehanna Health System, Regional Medical Center, Family Medicine Department at Williamsport Hospital and Medical Center
  • AEMC (Philadelphia): Emergency Medicine, OB/GYN, Pediatrics, Internal Medicine, and Family Medicine Departments
  • West Penn Allegheny Health System, Allegheny General Hospital, Emergency Department
  • Walter Reed National Military Medical Center, Preventive Medicine Department and Readiness Clinic

Population Served

  • The SMaRT Program serves patients in two major metropolitan areas (Pittsburgh and Philadelphia) and one nonmetropolitan area (Williamsport).
  • UPMC comprises more than 20 hospitals and numerous clinics and health care offices in western Pennsylvania. Within its various family medicine sites, patient demographics are reported as 34–37 percent males, 63–66 percent females, approximately 30 percent aged 19 or younger, 65 percent aged 19–59, and 5–9 percent aged 60 and older. Ethnicity is primarily White/Caucasian, followed by African American, Asian American, and Native American/Pacific Islander.
  • UPMC GIM patient demographics are as follows: approximately 62 percent females and 38 percent males; 35 percent aged 40 or younger, 47 percent aged 40–64, and 18 percent aged 65 or older; 68 percent Caucasian, 19 percent African American, 3 percent Asian/Asian American, 3 percent Asian/Pacific Islander, less than 1 percent Hispanic/Latino, less than 1 percent Native American Inuit, and 6 percent unknown/other.
  • AEMC, located in North Philadelphia, provides health care for a large, diverse, underserved population. According to the 2000 census, the area includes 322,455 residents. The community’s ethnic composition is reflected in its patient population: 68 percent African American, 13 percent Caucasian, 12 percent Hispanic, and 7 percent Asian; and approximately 63 percent females and 37 percent males. Patients seen in the Emergency Department are 3 percent aged 20 or younger, 90 percent aged 20–60, and 6 percent 60 and older.
  • The Williamsport Hospital Family Practice Residency Program, in north central Pennsylvania, encompasses the following patient demographics: 66 percent female and 34 percent male; 23 percent aged 19 or younger, 49 percent aged 20–59, and 25 percent aged 60 and older. Ethnicity is primarily White/Caucasian, followed by African American and Asian American.

Trainee Populations

Residents in six specialty areas

  • Family medicine
  • General internal medicine
  • Emergency medicine
  • OB/GYN
  • Pediatrics
  • Psychiatry
