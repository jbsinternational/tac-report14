Goals

  • Deliver an SBIRT training curriculum to five residency programs representing a diverse group of primary care residency programs, including university-based and other programs that serve urban, rural, and frontier populations throughout Oregon.
  • Create systems and organizational change in seven primary care clinics to promote and support the use of SBIRT practices.
  • Implement the use of SBIRT practices into routine clinical exams for all new patients and annually for existing patients.
  • Develop policies and procedures for disseminating the SBIRT curriculum and clinic practices to a broader network of university-based and other primary care programs throughout Oregon

Population Served

  • An estimated 150,000 Oregonians needed treatment for illicit drug use in 2005, and 345,000 needed treatment for alcohol abuse or dependence; however, only about 38 percent sought treatment from a public or private agency.
  • Oregon adults ranked fourth in the Nation for alcohol-related deaths and prescription drug use in 2005.
  • Oregon ranked seventh in the Nation for per capita methamphetamine abuse in 2005.
  • Approximately 30 percent of Oregon’s 3.2 million residents live in rural, sometimes remote, areas.
  • Although the residency is located in metropolitan Portland, 3 months of training during the 3 years of residency take place in rural communities.

Trainee Population

  • Approximately 400 physicians in 5 residency programs over 5 years
