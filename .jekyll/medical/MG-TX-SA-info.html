SBIRT-South Texas Area Residency Training (S-START)
University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
San Antonio, Texas

TI Number:
TI020249

Funding Period:
09/30/08–09/29/13

Grantee Project Director:
Janet F. Williams, M.D.
Tel: 210-562-5344
Email: jawilliams@uthscsa.edu

Visit the University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio SBIRT Website

Government Project Officer:
Reed Forman, M.S.W.

Partnering Organizations

  • University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio (UTHSCSA)
  • McAllen Family Medicine Residency Program (McAllen)
  • Regional Academic Health Center (RAHC) Internal Medicine Residency Program (Edinburg)
  • CHRISTUS Santa Rosa Health System, Family Medicine Program (San Antonio)
  • Fort Hood Army Base, Family Medicine Program (Killeen)
  • Brooke Army Medical Center, Adolescent Medicine Program (San Antonio)

Goals

  • Train UTHSCSA medical residents and residents from other participating south Texas programs to use evidence-based SBIRT procedures for patients who have or are at risk for substance use disorders.
  • Promote systems change in targeted residency programs by integrating the SBIRT model into the curriculum.
  • Enhance residents’ knowledge of evidence-based practices.
  • Enhance residents’ readiness and perceived confidence to implement SBIRT with their patients.
  • Increase residents’ implementation of SBIRT practices with their patients.
  • Enhance faculty participants’ SBIRT knowledge and confidence in their ability to teach SBIRT practices to future physicians.

Implementation Sites

  • UTHSCSA departments
    • Pediatrics
    • Family and Community Medicine
    • Internal Medicine
    • Obstetrics/Gynecology
    • Psychiatry (adult and child)
    • Surgery
  • RAHC Internal Medicine
  • McAllen Family Medicine
  • Clinics
    • Robert B. Green Campus–Downtown
    • Children’s Health Center
    • McAllen Family Medicine Residency Program Clinic

Population Served

  • The primary population served by S-START is Hispanic (75–95 percent); a large percentage speaks Spanish only.
  • Alcohol, tobacco, marijuana, cocaine, and heroin comprise the most prevalent substances addressed in clinical settings.

Trainee Populations

  • At least 679 residents and medical students over 5 years across UTHSCSA departments and diverse settings, including Fort Hood and Brooke Army Medical Center
