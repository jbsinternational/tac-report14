SBIRT Medical Residency Program at San Francisco General Hospital
San Francisco General Hospital

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
San Francisco, California

TI Number:
TI020296

Funding Period:
09/30/08–09/29/13

Grantee Project Director:
Paula Lum, M.D., M.P.H.
Tel: 415-476-4082 x411
Email: plum@php.ucsf.edu

Government Project Officer:
Reed Forman, M.S.W.

Partnering Organizations

  • San Francisco General Hospital (SFGH)
  • Community Behavioral Health Services, San Francisco Department of Public Health
  • New Mexico Public Health Association
  • Pediatric Academic Society

Goals

  • Develop and implement a training system to teach residents skills to provide evidence-based SBIRT at an urban county hospital serving a culturally diverse population affected by and at high risk for alcohol and drug use disorders.
  • Disseminate SBIRT practices widely by training across hospital departments and in local and statewide medical communitie

Implementation Sites

  • SFGH General Medical Clinic
  • Positive Health Program at SFGH
  • Family Health Center

Population Served

  • SFGH serves approximately 100,000 individuals each year, including people who are Hispanic (29 percent), Caucasian (25 percent), African American (21 percent), and Asian (20 percent). Many of them are socially vulnerable and marginalized.
  • A large percentage of patients are either publicly insured (80 percent) or uninsured (8 percent).
  • SFGH patients present a variety of health concerns, including HIV/AIDS, advanced disease, endemic violence, mental illness, and substance abuse and dependence.
  • Over the last decade, San Francisco has ranked among the top five cities in the United States for emergency department drug episodes. Heroin, cocaine, and methamphetamine abuse and dependence are the primary drivers of health issues.
  • Injection drug use has largely contributed to the high incidence of HIV in the city

Trainee Populations

  • University of California, San Francisco (UCSF), residents:
    • Primary Care Internal Medicine
    • Health Equity: Academics and Advocacy Training Area of Distinction
    • General Medical Clinic
    • Family and Community Medicine
    • Pediatrics (dissemination)
    • Pharmacy (dissemination)
  • Internal Medicine interns (dissemination)
