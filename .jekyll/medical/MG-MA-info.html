Training Residents in Adolescent SBIRT
Children’s Hospital Boston

  • Program Information
  • Site Visit Report

Location:
Boston, Massachusetts

TI Number:
TI020267

Funding Period:
09/30/08–09/29/13

Grantee Project Director:
Sharon Levy, M.D., M.P.H.
Tel: 857-218-4308
Email: Sharon.Levy@Childrens.Harvard.edu

Government Project Officer:
Erich Kleinschmidt, M.S.W., LICSW

Partnering Organizations

  • Boston-Combined Residency Program (BCRP) in Pediatrics, which includes the following:
    • Boston Medical Center (BMC): Division of Ambulatory Pediatrics and the Adolescent Medicine Center
    • Boston Children’s Hospital (BCH): Divisions of Adolescent Medicine, Developmental Medicine, Children’s Emergency Medicine, and Ambulatory Pediatrics
  • BCH Residency Program in Child and Adolescent Psychiatry
  • BMC New England Partnership for Substance Abuse Research
  • Massachusetts Department of Public Health
  • Portland, Maine, Department of Public Health
  • Association of Pediatric Program Directors
  • Pediatric Academic Societies
  • Association for Medical Education and Research in Substance Abuse
  • Society for Adolescent Medicine
  • Simmons College School of Social Work
  • Bouvé College of Health Sciences at Northeastern University, Pediatric Nurse Practitioner Program
  • Leadership in Adolescent Health at BCH
  • Division of Developmental Medicine at BCH

Goals

  • Develop a curriculum to teach pediatric clinicians SBIRT skills specific for adolescent patients.
  • Implement this curriculum with pediatric residents in the BCRP in Pediatrics and Child and Adolescent Psychiatry Residency at BCH.
  • Widely disseminate the curriculum and training materials to pediatrics and family medicine faculty through regional train-the-trainers workshops and nationally via the Internet

Implementation Sites

  • Four divisions in the Department of Medicine at BCH
    • Adolescent Medicine
    • Developmental Medicine
    • Children’s Emergency Medicine
    • Child Psychiatry
  • BCH Department of Psychiatry
  • BMC Division of Ambulatory Pediatrics, Adolescent Medicine Center
  • Clinical training sites, including the following:
    • Tufts Medical Center
    • Cambridge Pediatrics
    • Milton Family Practice
    • Capital Region Family Health Center

Population Served

  • Both BMC and BCH are located near large urban areas with many families living at or below the poverty level.
  • The two hospitals provide primary health care services to the majority of children in Boston and often function as community hospitals for families from some of the city’s poorest neighborhoods.
  • BMC is the largest provider of uncompensated care (Free Care) in Massachusetts. Children’s Hospital Primary Care Center is the largest provider of pediatric primary care to children in the city, and 65 percent are from inner-city neighborhoods.
  • The clinics serve various cultural and socioeconomic populations and age groups with diverse medical problems.

TRAINEE POPULATIONS

  • About 230 medical residents (46 per year for 5 years), including the following:
    • Approximately 205 pediatric first-year residents
    • Approximately 25 child psychiatry residents
  • Approximately 110 social workers
  • Social work faculty
  • Rotating medical students
  • Pediatric nurse practitioner students
