Implementation Sites

  • FQHCs in Polk County (central region), Black Hawk County (northeast region), Woodbury County (northwest region), and Scott County (southeast region)
    • Peoples Community Health Clinic (two sites in Blackhawk County)
    • Primary health care (10 clinic sites and a large homeless outreach program)
    • Siouxland Community Health Center (a clinic in Sioux City and a network of rural health clinics)
    • Community health care (six community health clinics in Davenport and a homeless outreach program)
  • Substance abuse/behavioral health providers
    • Pathways Behavioral Services (Waterloo)
    • Jackson Recovery Centers (Sioux City)
    • Center for Alcohol & Drug Services (Davenport)
    • Behavioral Management Services (Des Moines/Urbandale)
  • Iowa National Guard (Fort Dodge)

Population Served

  • The four FQHCs are located in regions with demonstrated community need consistent with prevalence data, incidence of alcohol-related illness and poverty, and potential impact.
  • Within the four FQHCs, 68 percent of patients are White, 23 percent are Hispanic, and 16 percent are African American.
  • Sixty-eight percent of patients live at or below 200 percent of the Federal poverty level; income levels for 28 percent are unknown.
  • The Iowa National Guard at Fort Dodge includes guardsmen returning from deployment. Its Army National Guard has been the second most deployed Guard to Iraq and Afghanistan during the past decade.
