  • September, 2012

    • Human Service Center

      September 27–28, 2012

      The Human Service Center’s Mobile Wellness and Recovery project provides individuals living within a 10-county region in rural central Illinois with access to a range of e-therapy options, community-based clinical services, and primary care to address their substance use issues. The project has partnered with North Central Behavioral Health Services and multiple primary care organizations to offer numerous media options, including smartphone applications, telephone communications, and Web-based interventions.

    October, 2012

    • WestCare Nevada, Inc.

      October 9–10, 2012

      WestCare Nevada is part of a larger, international nonprofit organization run by WestCare Foundation. WestCare Foundation provides a wide spectrum of health and human services in residential and outpatient environments. WestCare Nevada’s Rural Nevada Tele-Health Program delivers telehealth service expansion and enhancement in Nevada’s rural communities through Polycom.

    • Clinical and Support Options, Inc.

      October 15–16, 2012

      Clinical and Support Options, Inc. has implemented a technology-supported interactive system of outreach, intake, treatment, and aftercare services for rural adult residents with diagnosed substance use disorders living in three counties in western Massachusetts. The organization’s RECOVERe program uses mobile telephony, a Web site with personal portal access, and video conferencing technology for individual and group services.

    • First Call Alcohol/Drug Prevention and Recovery, Inc.

      October 22–23, 2012

      First Call's Mobile Motivational Enhancement Therapy program is designed to increase clients’ motivation for sustained engagement with treatment providers through the use of a Web-based, client-driven electronic health record. First Call has partnered with 26 behavioral health and social service agencies in Jackson County, Missouri, to deliver care to uninsured and underserved residents.

    • University of Kansas Medical Center

      October 23–24, 2012

      The University of Kansas Medical Center’s (KUMC) telehealth program is organized under the University of Kansas Center for Telemedicine and Telehealth Institute for Community Education within Kansas’ University Hospital system. KUMC’s health information technology program focuses on Web-based telemedicine to create a replicable, sustainable, and comprehensive substance abuse treatment model for rural colleges. The program has a strong prevention focus, including prevention education through Web-based strategies such as social media and virtual online environments (e.g., Second Life).

    November, 2012

    • Central Oklahoma Family Medical Center

      November 1–2, 2012

      The Central Oklahoma Family Medical Center (COFMC) is a federally qualified health center that provides outpatient substance abuse treatment services to rural, underserved residents of select counties in south-central Oklahoma. COFMC has adapted its electronic health record system (NextGen) to integrate primary care with substance abuse treatment services at two participating locations.

    March, 2013

    • Spectrum Programs, Inc.

      March 26–27, 2013

      Spectrum Program, Inc. (SPI), has provided more than 30 years of substance abuse recovery services in Miami-Dade and Broward counties. SPI is also part of Banyan Health Systems, a comprehensive primary care and behavioral health provider. SPI’s Electronic Transitions program provides services to women aged 25–34 who abuse prescription and nonprescription drugs. Intervention services are supported through e-therapy, recovery coaching, a Wellness Recovery Action Plan, and an electronic curriculum.

    April, 2013

    • River Edge Behavioral Health

      April 23–24, 2013

      River Edge Behavioral Health Center is a 63-year-old provider agency with services in mostly rural locations. It has implemented telehealth through VeaMea, I-Care Recovery Network (a Web portal), and Smoke Signal (a reminder system). River Edge provides many services: Assertive Community Treatment; adult day services; adult outpatient services; child, adolescent, and family services; community support services; crisis intervention services; developmental services; pharmacy services; outpatient services for women with children; recovery centers; supported employment; and prevention resources.
