Population Served

  • Uninsured and underinsured individuals aged 18 and over who reside in Jackson County, Missouri, and are accessing services from among 26 community behavioral health and mental health agencies funded by the Jackson County Community-backed Anti-drug Tax (COMBAT)

Project Goals

  • Improved client access to treatment and increased client engagement with providers by—
    • Developing a mobile platform for the client portal
    • Designing, developing, and integrating an interface with ASI-MV for future partnerships and use
    • Training participating agency staff
    • Marketing the client portal
    • Expanding the provider network
    • Including product enhancements

Grantee Evaluation

Strengths
  • First Call’s capacity for data collection is impressive because the EHR allows detailed client information to be collected for several years.
  • Reposting capabilities within the EHR application allow for the generation of on-demand reports. Its database can also be utilized by statistical analytic software.
  • There is a high degree of interoperability which allows for connection to State-mandated databases and e-Health Align, the area HIE.
Challenges
  • None noted
