Strengths and Considerations for Action

Program Vision and Design

Strengths

  • Increase the numbers of rural college students seen for substance abuse treatment using telemedicine and provide a high level of satisfaction across clients.

Challenges

  • The program has a strong prevention focus; however, the grant project’s focus is on employing technology in the delivery of substance abuse treatment services.

Grantee Leadership

Strengths

  • None noted.

Challenges

  • None noted.

Implementation Plan

Strengths

  • KUMC has the ability to deploy telehealth using flat screens, smaller units (desktops), and iPads.
  • Utilize Second Life to provide prevention, education, and training material to UKMC’s medical and nursing students and to Pittsburg State University students in substance abuse education.
  • Partnership with Central Kansas Foundation (CKF) includes CKF providing the substance abuse and mental health treatment services with KUMC providing the telehealth equipment (Polycom).

Challenges

  • Extensive requirements for collection of data at intake, lead to the decrease in client engagement, thus leading to delaying the initiation of treatment.
  • Workflow challenges affect program participation from other college entities.
  • Security issues exist for the Second Life site and telehealth equipment.
  • Hiring staff to run the program is a challenge.
  • Relying primarily on college students in the longer term treatment model does not allow the program to receive sufficient numbers of clients needed to meet the target.
  • College students are generally a transient population, making it difficult to do followups at 6 month.

Community Linkages, Partners, and Participation

Strengths

  • Partnership with CKF includes CKF providing the substance abuse and mental health treatment services, while UKMC provides the telehealth equipment.
  • The KUCTT program is an arm of the university’s telecommunication department, which has already utilized its technology in medical specialties such as pediatrics and cardiology.

Challenges

  • There are not enough partnerships with colleges to sustain the program.

Client Outreach, Recruitment, and Referral

Strengths

  • Referrals are provided by the health clinic, judicial officer violations, student conduct committee, and the athletic department when drug testing results in a positive finding.
  • Students who are referred to the counseling program have different levels of completion requirements and the potential for consequences if not completed.

Challenges

  • Difficulties in relationship building affect a process for obtaining referrals.
  • Delays exist in receiving referrals from the participating college’s sanction process because of the academic calendar and school policies.
  • There is no strong marketing plan in place.

Sustainability Planning

Strengths

  • As a large hospital system, there is already a third-party billing system in place that bills for Medicaid, Medicare, self-paid, and private insurance.

Challenges

  • Difficulties exist in obtaining partnerships with rural colleges.
  • There is no plan for sustainability.

Grantee Evaluation

Strengths

  • The program collects currently required documentation at intake including Government Performance and Results Act (GPRA), the Substance Abuse Subtle Screening Inventory (SASSI), and CKF’s State-mandated screening assessment, record of consult, and three different patient privacy and security forms.

Challenges

  • There is no defined evaluator position or staff member to direct evaluation projects. The other staff members with evaluation experience are taking over the responsibilities.
  • The documentation requirements are too cumbersome and decreasing the potential for quickly engaging the client.
