  • April, 2013

    • Promesa Behavioral Health

      April 17–18, 2013

      Promesa Behavioral Health (PBH) focuses on youth, providing outpatient substance abuse services, group homes, and infant care. Since 2009, PBH has provided telecare-based treatment services to clients outside the headquarters area. PBH’s program Telecare Outpatient Alcohol and Drug Services provides behavioral health services with interactive consultation among patient, psychiatrist, and addiction counselors. Services are supported by various technologies, including text messaging, instant messaging, videoconferencing, and electronic health records.

    May, 2013

    • Buffalo Valley, Inc.

      May 21–22, 2013

      Buffalo Valley, Inc. (BVI), provides technology-based substance abuse services to rural, underserved individuals residing in Middle Tennessee, including disabled veterans, individuals who are homeless, and those involved with the criminal justice system. BVI’s Connect program is offered at five licensed treatment sites in the area.

    • Irene Stacy Community Mental Health Center

      May 30–31, 2013

      The Irene Stacy Community Mental Health Center provides outpatient and residential services to underserved individuals, including veterans who struggle with mental illness, intellectual disabilities, and substance use disorders. The program employs the Anazazi software, a behavioral health application, to assist in scheduling and billing operations.

    July, 2013

    • Forest County Potawatomi Health & Wellness Center

      July 10–11, 2013

      The Forest County Potawatomi Health and Wellness Center uses the patient-centric model of care. The facility is multidisciplinary and houses a variety of departments. The center serves the Potawatomi tribe and the nonnative rural community of Crandon. Telehealth services are provided through Polycom. Recovery and treatment efforts are supported not only through technology, but also via recovery coaches and by incorporating native culture and spiritual beliefs into treatment.

    August, 2013

    • First Choice Health Services, Inc.

      August 27–28, 2013

      First Choice Health Services, Inc., launched the Appalachian Technology-Assisted Recovery Innovations (ATARI) program to address the substance abuse treatment needs of low-income and medically underserved West Virginians struggling with substance abuse and mental health issues. The ATARI program has been integrated into nine rurally located behavioral health centers throughout the State.

    September, 2013

    • Iowa Department of Public Health

      September 10−11, 2013

      The Iowa Department of Public Health (IDPH) is the only State grant recipient in the TAC portfolio. Its Iowa Recovery Health Information Technology program was designed to increase the State’s ability to reach, engage, and retain rural clients in need of intensive outpatient substance abuse services through the use of culturally competent, clinically appropriate, and evidence-based technology. IDPH serves Iowa’s 99 counties and the nearly 23,000 clients seeking substance abuse treatment services at 23 publicly funded facilities.
