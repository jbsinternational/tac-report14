TCE-TAC Program

The purpose of the Targeted Capacity Expansion Technology-Assisted Care (TCE-TAC) program is to leverage technology that will enhance and expand provider capacity to offer substance abuse treatment options to underserved populations. The targeted individuals may lack access to treatment as a result of transportation barriers, a shortage of substance abuse treatment providers in the community, and/or financial constraints. The use of health information technology, including Web-based services, smartphones, and behavioral health electronic applications (e-apps), enhances the ability of providers to effectively communicate with persons in treatment and to track and manage client health to ensure services are available where and when needed. The program is designed to address gaps in substance abuse prevention and treatment services and increase the ability of States, local governments, American Indian/Alaska Native tribes and tribal organizations, and community- and faith-based organizations to serve their communities.

TCE-TAC Target Populations

The TCE-TAC program serves the following target populations:

  • Homeless individuals in rural areas and those with barriers to accessing treatment
  • Latino, African American, Native American, and White individuals with co-occurring substance use and mental illness disorders  
  • Persons with comorbid substance abuse and chronic health conditions and prescription medication abuse and misuse
  • Pregnant women with substance use disorders
  • Veterans
  • Persons involved with the criminal justice system
  • Individuals who are deaf and hard of hearing
