University of Missouri-Kansas City (UMKC) School of Nursing and Health Studies

PROGRAM VISION AND DESIGN

Strengths

  • UMKC SBIRT staff appear dedicated to integrating behavioral health in medical settings.
  • UMKC SBIRT has clear goals and objectives.
  • The grantee has made significant modifications to the SBIRT curriculum to make it more relevant to nurses and social workers.

Challenges

  • None noted

GRANTEE LEADERSHIP

Strengths

  • Program staff appear experienced, dedicated, and competent.
  • The council of directors (COD) is diverse and dedicated to the project.

Challenges

  • COD members have concerns about long-term sustainability of the COD.
  • The project director has many responsibilities, so leading the COD could be too taxing.

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Strengths

  • The grantee is flexible and willing to modify the program.
  • The grantee is ahead of most grantees with regard to training and implementation.
  • The faculty and preceptor training is thorough.

Challenges

  • The thoroughness of training could place too much burden on the UMKC SBIRT training staff.
  • The grantee would like more diverse training videos.

COMMUNITY LINKAGES, PARTNERS, AND PARTICIPATION

Strengths

  • The grantee has a large and diverse partner network that includes members within UMKC and at different clinical sites in the area.
  • The grantee’s dissemination plan is ambitious and already being implemented.

Challenges

  • It is difficult to combine training for nursing and social work students.

EXPERIENTIAL LEARNING

Strengths

  • The grantee has an extensive experiential learning program in which all students and faculty have access to standardized patients.

Challenge

  • Training preceptors and encouraging clinical sites to implement SBIRT is extremely challenging.

AFFORDABLE CARE ACT READINESS

Strengths

  • The grantee is addressing billing and coding for SBIRT.
  • The grantee is preparing a workforce (both nurses and social workers) that will integrate behavioral health in clinical settings.

Challenge

  • None noted.

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Strengths

  • SBIRT has been well integrated into the various UMKC programs.
  • UMKC SBIRT has many supporters and strong buy-in from both UMKC faculty and community partners.

Challenge

  • A standout component of the program is its extensive use of standardized patients, though they are very expensive.
  • Most of the faculty members are being paid through the SBIRT grant, which could prove problematic when the grant ends.

GRANTEE EVALUATION

Strengths

  • The evaluation plan is clear, concise, and realistic.

Challenge

  • Students can be difficult to contact once they have graduated.

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Grantee Leadership
    • To sustain the COD, consider setting clear goals and objectives for the group and working groups for specific functions and topics.
    • Consider formulating a leadership group within the COD and electing a chairperson.
  • Implementation Plan
    • While the faculty training is excellent, consider a less intense training regimen for course instructors.
  • Experiential Learning
    • As planned, train 180 preceptors and advocate for the sites where students are in clinical placements to implement SBIRT.
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Offer alternatives to using standardized patients, which might be too costly to sustain.
    • Consider putting in steps and processes so the SBIRT program can run on “autopilot” when funding ends.
    • Find a champion willing to monitor and update the program periodically with little to no funding.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Consider following up with students postgraduation through survey services, such as SurveyMonkey, and collecting contact data before students leave the program.

Potential Technical Assistance Requests

  • Implementation Plan
    • Obtain more diversity in actors in training videos and a video of a nurse conducting a brief negotiated interview with a patient who has risky or harmful marijuana use.
  • Community Linkages, Partners, and Participation
    • Bring in a high-profile speaker/trainer for the next meeting even though the grantee has much buy-in. The staff desire to expand the reach and bring in more supporters.
