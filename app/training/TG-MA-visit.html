April 8–9, 2014

Site Visit Team Members:
Consulting Staff: Joseph Hyde, M.A., LMHC; Beth Johnson, M.P.H.
Peer Consultant: Janet Williams, M.D., FAAP

Sites Visited:
Boston Children’s Hospital, Adolescent Substance Abuse Program Clinic

PROGRAM VISION AND DESIGN

Strengths

  • The grantee views SBIRT as an important part of core skills to be taught to health care providers serving adolescents and their families.

Challenges

  • The grantee will need to ensure the SBIRT training fits within the various programs’ philosophies, missions, and existing curricula.  

GRANTEE LEADERSHIP

Strengths

  • The Boston Children’s Hospital (BCH) SBIRT team members are highly experienced scholars, educators, and practitioners serving an adolescent population.
  • The participating site directors from the schools of social work and nursing are committed to building capacity within their schools to give students an opportunity to learn about substance use and SBIRT.
  • Membership on the council of directors includes leadership from within BCH and participating schools of nursing and social work.

Challenges

  • Faculty from the schools of social work and nursing appear to be at varying levels of knowledge, readiness, and buy-in regarding SBIRT.

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Strengths

  • The grantee is building on previous success as a SAMHSA Medical Residency grantee and has institutional knowledge and existing relationships that have assisted in a successful startup.
  • The grantee has a large body of existing training materials to apply to this initiative.

Challenges

Social work and nursing field placement sites operate differently from medical residency programs. The field placements are greater in number and operate in a larger geographic area. The following are viewed as challenges:

  • Coordination and communication of expectations between schools and multiple field placements
  • Building SBIRT-related knowledge and skills of field instructors and supervisors
  • Working with field-placement agencies to ensure interns have an adequate opportunity to practice SBIRT skills
  • Establishing a uniform protocol to measure and document proficiency with the SBIRT intervention

COMMUNITY LINKAGES, PARTNERS, AND PARTICIPATION

Strengths

  • BCH and its partners have extensive ties to key institutions and organizations statewide and nationally.

Challenges

  • None noted

CLIENT OUTREACH, RECRUITMENT, AND REFERRAL

Strengths

  • All SBIRT patient activities are delivered through the practice sites of the participating training programs.

Challenges

  • The grantee indicated concerns about social work and nursing trainees having the opportunity to practice SBIRT skills in field placements.

AFFORDABLE CARE ACT READINESS

Strengths

  • The grantee is supporting the next generation of health care providers to learn the skills and practices to be used in the future health care delivery system.

Challenge

  • None noted.

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Strengths

  • The grantee is embedding the SBIRT curriculum within existing curricula.
  • The grantee is grooming champions across disciplines.

Challenges

  • Building the capacity of field placements to support, reinforce, and align with training experiences is viewed as key to trainees’ incorporating these skills and using SBIRT in their future practice.

GRANTEE EVALUATION

Strengths

  • The grantee has a well-developed plan for process and outcome evaluation.

Challenges

  • None noted.

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Program Vision and Design
    • Have the council of directors play a vital role in adopting SBIRT across disciplines.
  • Grantee Leadership
    • Continue to build faculty readiness and buy-in through training and information dissemination.
    • Enhance access to the SAMHSA SBIRT online series as an additional and no-cost strategy to increase faculty knowledge.
  • Implementation Plan
    • Consider developing a strategy to build SBIRT proficiency with field instructors, ensure trainees have an opportunity to practice SBIRT skills, and establish a protocol to assess trainee proficiency.
  • Client Outreach, Recruitment, and Referral
    • Consider working with field placement agencies to ensure trainees have adequate opportunities to practice SBIRT skills.
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Consider developing a strategy for building the capacity of social work and nursing field placements.

Potential Technical Assistance Requests

  • Implementation Plan
    • Consider developing a strategy to build SBIRT proficiency with field instructors, ensure trainees have an opportunity to practice SBIRT skills, and establish a protocol to assess trainee proficiency.
    • Receive information and potential technical assistance regarding evidence-based brief treatment, as suggested by the site director at the School of Social Work. 
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Consider developing a strategy for building the capacity of social work and nursing field placements.
