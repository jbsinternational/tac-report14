Goals

  • Build the capacity of Indiana’s health care professionals to provide SBIRT services through education.
  • Promote systematic educational change at Indiana University.

Implementation Sites

  • Indiana University School of Medicine—Residency programs in internal medicine and medicine-pediatrics
  • Indiana University School of Nursing—Graduate students’ clinical practicum venues
    • Community, migrant, and rural health centers
    • Local and State health departments
    • Prisons
    • Schools
    • Other areas where underserved populations are the focus of care
  • Indiana University School of Social Work—Program places advanced clinical masters of social work (MSW) interns in a variety of settings with diverse populations
    • Behavioral health clinics
    • Primary care clinics
    • Hospitals
    • Substance abuse clinics
    • Schools
    • Residential facilities
    • Family preservation and reunification
    • Child services
    • The statewide Veterans Affairs system

Population Served

  • Indiana has a significantly higher percentage of admissions for substance abuse treatment due to alcohol (47 percent) than the entire U.S. population (39 percent) (Indiana Family and Social Services Administration, 2007).
  • Middle and high school students in Indianapolis, Indiana, have higher lifetime alcohol use rates than the State and national averages (Drug Free Marion County, 2011).
  • Indiana has nearly 227,000 adults living with a serious mental illness and approximately 71,000 children with serious mental health conditions (National Alliance on Mental Illness, 2011).

Trainee Populations

  • Approximately 50 medical residents
  • Approximately 60 nurse practitioner students
