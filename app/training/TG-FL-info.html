Goals

  • Achieve buy-in and adoption from nursing students, health professionals, and leadership at university schools and practice sites on the benefits of SBIRT in integrating behavioral health and primary care and better serving patients by helping them identify and treat unhealthy substance use behaviors.
  • Develop and finalize the infrastructure, partnerships, processes, curricula, training plans, and performance monitoring system to ensure timely and complete implementation.
  • Increase the number of nurses, residents, counseling students, nursing students, and social work students trained in SBIRT.
  • Increase the number of nurses, residents, counseling students, nursing students, and social work students who are competent in motivational interviewing and able to provide more intensive brief treatment.
  • Create collaborative, multidisciplinary teams at the University of Miami Hospital, St. John Bosco Clinic, and Connect Familias community partnership that support implementation of SBIRT in practice settings and serve as catalysts for systems change.
  • Create a seamless network between specialized treatment programs and early intervention teams that cuts across the health care system.
  • Conduct workforce development activities across the State of Florida, with a focus on SBIRT, motivational interviewing, and the integration of behavioral health and primary care.
  • Collect de-identified data on patients who interact with participants of the SBIRT trainings at the University of Miami Hospital, St. John Bosco, and Connect Familias.

Population Served

  • Miami-Dade County, the most ethnically diverse county in South Florida, achieved “majority minority” status in 2010. Its population (2,500,625) is 62.5 percent Hispanic and 19.5 percent Black (Miami Dade County, 2010; U.S. Census Bureau, 2010).
  • The heterogeneity of the minority population in South Florida provides an opportunity for better understanding minority subgroups, including a large population of sexual minorities.
  • Jackson Memorial Hospital is the main source of indigent care in South Florida. Jefferson Reaves, Sr., Health Center is a focal point for health care services in an underserved area with high levels of crime and poverty.
