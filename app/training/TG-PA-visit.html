PROGRAM VISION AND DESIGN

STRENGTHS

  • The Allegheny-Singer Research Institute (ASRI) and the Allegheny Health Network (AHN) have a clearly defined mission to effectively train medical professional trainees and faculty members so they can proficiently practice SBIRT in any clinical site where they work.

  • ASRI and AHN envision that trainees will promote and disseminate SBIRT practices throughout the Pennsylvania health care community.

CHALLENGES

  • None noted.

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Strengths

  • The program is fully implemented in all disciplines and incorporates multiple learning strategies.
  • The program has adopted strategies to support trainee practice within clinical settings, including use of preceptors/champions and a proficiency tool supporting fidelity.

Challenge

  • The grant application proposed training the behavioral health workforce in brief treatment, but this has not occurred.

COMMUNITY LINKAGES, PARTNERS, AND PARTICIPATION

Strengths

  • The grantee is embedded within a large, integrated health system that offers ample opportunity to practice the SBIRT intervention and study team-oriented models of service delivery.

Challenge

  • None noted.

AFFORDABLE CARE ACT READINESS

Strengths

  • Trainees will learn and work within a health care delivery system that is aligning operations with the newest models of care.

Challenge

  • None noted.

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Strengths

  • The grantee has developed specific course content in collaboration with champions and course instructors to promote buy-in, ownership, and sustainability.
  • The grantee is working to integrate SBIRT within the AHN electronic health record systems.
  • The council of directors will support sustainability planning efforts.

Challenges

  • None noted.

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Implementation Plan
    • Consider including social work and psychology interns in the training program.
    • Consider training behavioral health trainees in brief treatment approaches.
  • Community Linkages, Partners, and Participation
    • Consider developing training modules on team-oriented, integrated approaches to the delivery of SBIRT.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Consider conducting focus groups 6 to 12 months posttraining to gather information about the implementation successes, problems, and issues that arise when trainees are in practice. This information would complement the evaluation data already collected and could further support training and clinical programs.
