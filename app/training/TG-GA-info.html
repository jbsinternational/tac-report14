Goals

  • Equip APRN students, faculty, and preceptors with the skills needed to provide evidence-based, culturally competent screening, brief intervention, brief treatment, and referral to specialty treatment for patients with unhealthy substance use.
  • Promote use of SBIRT in clinical practice through competency certification of trainees, instruction in billing and coding, regional dissemination workshops, and advocacy for advances in training and reimbursement.

Population Served

  • APRN specialties span the gamut of U.S. health care, providing care across the range of acuity of patients and the entire lifespan.
  • The largest proportion of APRNs is trained for primary care settings—family nurse practitioners, adult primary care, pediatrics, and women’s health.
  • Approximately 84 percent of graduating APRNs enter the primary care workforce.

Trainee Populations

  • Approximately 72 faculty
  • Approximately 120 preceptors
