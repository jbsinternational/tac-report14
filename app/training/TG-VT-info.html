Goals

  • Generate the infrastructure to provide team-based training and care to family medicine residents, internal medicine residents, and nursing, social work, and counseling students.
  • Introduce the SBIRT training curriculum to students in each discipline.
  • Disseminate the training model and content across the Vermont health care system.
  • Conduct an evaluation to assess the reach, effectiveness, adoption, implementation, and maintenance of project implementation, training, and systems redesign.

Implementation Sites

  • Programs of the University of Vermont SBIRT Training Collaborative

    • Family medicine and internal medicine residency programs
    • Department of Social Work
    • College of Nursing
    • Counseling program

Population Served

There are a total of 225 health care professionals enrolled in postgraduate education in residency or graduate programs (3 yearly cohorts of 75 each), including the following:

  • Approximately 45 counseling students
  • Approximately 18 family medicine residents
  • Approximately 42 medical residents
  • Approximately 75 nurse practitioner students
  • Approximately 45 social work students
