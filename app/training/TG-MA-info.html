  • Simmons College School of Social Work
  • Massachusetts Department of Public Health

Goals

  • Provide SBIRT training to nurses, social workers, and subspecialty physicians who care for adolescent patients.
  • Optimize adoption and use of adolescent SBIRT.
  • Extend medical professional knowledge of SBIRT, expand workforce capacity for undertaking it effectively, and advance its sustainable institutionalization within diverse practice settings.

Implementation Sites

  • DDM—Clinical services at BCH
  • Bouvé College, School of Nursing Master of Science Pediatric Nurse Practitioner (PNP) Program—Clinical placements at BCH, Boston Medical Center, Massachusetts General Hospital, and Franciscan Hospital for Children
  • Simmons College School of Social Work, master of social work (MSW) program—More than 300 field placement sites throughout New England ranging from schools and hospitals to government agencies and private organizations, including BCH, Boston Medical Center, Boston public schools, and Massachusetts General Hospital
  • Boston LeAh—Clinical services through the adolescent/young adult medical practice at BCH and satellite clinics in the greater Boston area

Population Served

  • The programs provide clinical services to underserved communities and at-risk pediatric populations via relationships with community health centers, school-based clinics, therapeutic schools, and homeless shelters.
  • BCH and other field placement partners provide primary health care services to children living in Boston. BCH often functions as a community hospital for families from the poorest neighborhoods in the city, and it serves as a referral center for patients with the most complex medical and behavioral disorders.
  • The DDM annually serves 2,200 adolescents who have medical, developmental, and/or behavioral problems across a full spectrum. Such patients are vulnerable to the acute and chronic consequences of substance use.
  • The PNP program serves children and adolescents living in urban settings who are at risk across the continuum of care.
  • The MSW students work with adolescents in medical settings, schools, and integrated primary care and behavioral health settings.
  • The LeAH curriculum focuses on adolescents with serious substance use disorders. Its clinical services partners serve over 4,800 adolescents, aged 12–22, with more than 19,000 visits annually in the Greater Boston area.

Trainee Populations

A total of 480 trainees are projected:

  • Approximately 36 pediatric nurse practitioners per year (108 total)
  • Approximately 10–15 MSW per year (30–45 total)
  • Approximately 8–10 LeAH fellows per year (24–30 total)
  • Approximately 1–3 DDM fellows (3–9 total)
  • Approximately 300 school nurses
