Sites Visited:
National Catholic School of Social Service                    

PROGRAM VISION AND DESIGN

Strengths

  • The project is using the SAMHSA core curriculum to tailor selected courses.

Challenges

  • The number of implementation sites poses a challenge because many preceptors and field instructors throughout the city need to be trained. 

GRANTEE LEADERSHIP

Strengths

  • The project has leadership from both the National Catholic School of Social Service (NCSSS) and School of Nursing (SON).

Challenges

  • The project director is on the project only 20 percent of her time.

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Strengths

  • Training sites are varied throughout the District of Columbia area.
  • Project staffs have existing, longstanding relationships with community preceptors.

Challenges

  • The curriculum is still being developed.

COMMUNITY LINKAGES, PARTNERS, AND PARTICIPATION

Strengths

  • NCSSS and SON have established relationships working with clinical agencies in the community.

Challenges

  • None noted

CLIENT OUTREACH, RECRUITMENT, AND REFERRAL

Strength

  • • The project will make Web-based modules, training manuals, and teaching materials available outside the program to expand SBIRT outreach.

Challenge

  • None noted.

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Strength

  • The Kanbar facility and staff are supported by an endowment from UCSF, not by grant funds, making the facility a sustainable resource for the UCSF SBIRT program after the grant ends.

Challenges

  • None noted

AFFORDABLE CARE ACT READINESS

Strengths

  • The project is training the next generation of the health care workforce in the skills and practices necessary to successfully adapt to the changing health care delivery system.

Challenges

  • None noted

SUSTAINABILITY PLANNING

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Buy-in and commitment have been obtained from the professors and clinical faculty across all levels in the various disciplines.

Challenges

  • The instruments used are long and therefore time-consuming to complete.

RECOMMENDED ENHANCEMENTS

Grantee-Led Enhancements

  • Program Vision and Design
    • Consider focusing on implementation in a few sites to increase buy-in among preceptors and field instructors.
  • Grantee Leadership
    • Consider increasing the level of effort for the project director.
  • Implementation Plan
    • Consider co-teaching the courses with faculty to ensure fidelity to the SBIRT model.
    • Create champions within the faculty to increase buy-in and sustainability.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Consider a proficiency checklist to share with field instructors and clinical preceptors.
    • Consider reducing the size of the instruments used.

Potential Technical Assistance Requests

  • Implementation Plan
    • Work with the council of directors and faculty to identify gaps in curriculum learning.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Consider a proficiency checklist to share with field instructors and clinical preceptors.
