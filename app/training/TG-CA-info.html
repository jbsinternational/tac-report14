Goals

  • Develop and deliver an SBIRT training program that will teach the necessary skills to health professional students from a variety of disciplines.
  • Increase the adoption and practice of SBIRT throughout Los Angeles health care systems.
  • Incorporate SBIRT training permanently into the educational curriculum of identified health care professional programs.
  • Create a working network between stakeholders in the Los Angeles health care systems and substance abuse treatment programs to accelerate the rate of engagement into treatment and strengthen linkages.
  • Expand the existing and future workforce capability to conduct and grow SBIRT practices throughout various health care treatment systems by advancing the professional development of health care students.

Population Served

  • The clinical rotations are at institutions serving large and diverse populations, including vulnerable patients who are underserved, medically indigent, low income, uninsured, homeless, victims of intimate partner violence, and persons with HIV/AIDS.
  • Some focus on infants, children, and adolescents, while the VA-WLA focuses on veterans.
  • The majority of the patient population that Los Angeles SBIRT graduates work with is similar to the population they worked with during clinical training.

Trainee Populations

A minimum of 325 trainees per year (975 total) include the following:

  • Approximately 50 UCLA residents across different medical specialties
  • Approximately 75 UCLA nursing students
  • Approximately 150 USC social work students
  • Approximately 50 California Association of Alcoholism and Drug Abuse counselors
  • An additional 184 UCLA medical students will be trained.
