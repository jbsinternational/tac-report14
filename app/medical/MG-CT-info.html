Goals

  • Expand and adapt the SBIRT curriculum for multiple practitioners and specialties.
  • Modify microsystems in practice sites to ensure long-term adoption of SBIRT.
  • Incorporate effective communication and cultural competency.
  • Develop enduring instructional strategies and disseminate teaching materials, tools, and other resources.

Population Served

  • According to 2006 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System data, Connecticut ranks in the top 20 percent for use of marijuana, cocaine, and nonmedical use of pain relievers for persons aged 18–25 ( 15.2 percent).
  • New Haven, the third largest city in Connecticut, has approximately 123,626 residents. It ranks among the poorest cities in the United States. Approximately 24 percent of residents live below the poverty level, compared with 7.9 percent of Connecticut residents and 12.4 percent in the Nation.
  • New Haven’s racial composition is 43.5 percent White, 37.4 percent Black, and 21.4 percent Hispanic or Latino (of any race).
  • The majority of YNHH patients are female, minority, and receiving care under entitlements (Medicare and Medicaid).
