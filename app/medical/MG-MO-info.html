Goals

  • Expand the availability and quality of medical care in SBIRT for individuals with hazardous or harmful alcohol use, misuse of prescription medications, or use of illicit drugs.
  • Develop curricula, teaching strategies, and sustainable infrastructure for training faculty, residents, and medical and nursing students in the effective use of SBIRT in six residency programs at the MU School of Medicine, Interdisciplinary Center on Aging, and Sinclair School of Nursing.
  • Develop tools and processes to successfully integrate SBIRT into routine care in outpatient and inpatient clinical settings in both schools and across all seven medical residency programs.
  • Disseminate SBIRT training, tools, and processes to nurses, physicians, and other clinicians in community practice, and facilitate sustainable implementation in their routine work.
  • Foster collaborative relationships between physicians and specialized alcohol and drug treatment providers to facilitate referrals and ensure a continuum of care that meets all levels of need.
  • Collect and present compelling data demonstrating SBIRT’s positive impact in these settings to support advocacy for broad system changes in health policy and funding.
  • Facilitate policy changes that sustain SBIRT training in medical schools and residencies and integration in community practice.
  • Facilitate policy changes that increase availability of specialized drug and alcohol treatment.

Population Served

  • Approximately 88 percent of the patients in central Missouri medical practices are White, 9 percent are African American, 1 percent are American Indian, and 2 percent are Asian; 1 percent are Hispanic or Latino (of any race).
  • The program serves patients from early adolescence to the elderly.
  • Patients include three major high-risk populations: pregnant women, trauma patients, and patients with co-occurring serious mental illnesses.

Trainee Populations

Over the 5-year period, MU’s SBIRT program expects to train the following numbers of individuals:

  • Approximately 360 residents (full curriculum)
  • Approximately 1,500 nursing students
  • Approximately 200 nurses
  • Approximately 980 medical students
  • Approximately 180 social work students
  • Up to 300 practicing physicians and other health care professionals (regional and statewide trainings)

These individuals are drawn from several programs:

  • Residents and medical students at MU School of Medicine
  • Graduate nursing students at MU Sinclair School of Nursing
  • Residents rotating through MU Department of Emergency Medicine
  • Fellows and other providers at MU Interdisciplinary Center on Aging
  • Community clinicians and residency programs beyond MU
