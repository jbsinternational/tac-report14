Goals

  • Train UTHSCSA medical residents and residents from other participating south Texas programs to use evidence-based SBIRT procedures for patients who have or are at risk for substance use disorders.
  • Promote systems change in targeted residency programs by integrating the SBIRT model into the curriculum.
  • Enhance residents’ knowledge of evidence-based practices.
  • Enhance residents’ readiness and perceived confidence to implement SBIRT with their patients.
  • Increase residents’ implementation of SBIRT practices with their patients.
  • Enhance faculty participants’ SBIRT knowledge and confidence in their ability to teach SBIRT practices to future physicians.

Population Served

  • The primary population served by S-START is Hispanic (75–95 percent); a large percentage speaks Spanish only.
  • Alcohol, tobacco, marijuana, cocaine, and heroin comprise the most prevalent substances addressed in clinical settings.

Trainee Populations

  • At least 679 residents and medical students over 5 years across UTHSCSA departments and diverse settings, including Fort Hood and Brooke Army Medical Center
