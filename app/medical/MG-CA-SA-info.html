Goals

  • Develop (and evaluate for cultural competency) a curriculum that applies the SBIRT core competencies to the general population with emphasis on three highly vulnerable populations: Latino youth, homeless individuals, and Latino seniors.
  • Promote adoption of SBIRT methodologies through the delivery of training to community-based agency staff and clients as well as local medical communities for wider dissemination of SBIRT practices.
  • Promote adoption of SBIRT methodologies through outcomes evaluation and dissemination of the results to the medical community.
  • Transition the project to a fully sustainable, permanent component of the residency program.
  • Develop materials to support dissemination to other SBIRT programs that may serve similar populations.

Population Served

  • The total Monterey County population is 410,206, and the Hispanic population is expected to increase to more than 300,000 by 2020. Approximately 70 percent of Natividad inpatients in 2006 were Hispanic/Latino.
  • Monterey County has a large prison population, resulting in the drug use and abuse issues often associated with that population.
  • Monterey County has experienced an increase in drug-related deaths across all populations since 1998, and the rate among 34- to 65-year-olds has nearly tripled.
  • Natividad Family Medicine patients have serious problems of substance abuse, including a high incidence of prenatal exposure among young mothers, overdosing among young adults, and a large population of homeless people with serious addictions. Distinct subpopulations include Latino youth, seniors, and homeless individuals.

Trainee Populations

  • At least 40 Natividad Family Practice residents over a 3-year period
  • University of California, San Francisco, Natividad third-year medical students
