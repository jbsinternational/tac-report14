Goals

  • Train physicians to provide SBIRT services and to promote systemic change in residency programs by integrating SBIRT into the curriculum on a long-term basis.
  • Develop exportable models of how to integrate SBIRT services into comprehensive outpatient clinic settings by involving allied health professionals and using information technology and conventional tool

Implementation Sites

  • Indiana University (IU) Medical Center campus, Indianapolis: Schools of Medicine, Nursing, Social Work, and Public and Environmental Affairs
  • IUSM network of hospitals, including University Hospital, Methodist Hospital, Riley Hospital for Children, and suburban hospitals (IU Health West Hospital and IU Health North Hospital)
  • IUSM Centers for Medical Education
    • Ball Memorial Hospital, Muncie
    • St. Vincent’s Hospital, Indianapolis
    • Centers in Gary, Terre Haute, Muncie, Fort Wayne, Bloomington, Evansville, South Bend, and Lafayette
  • Primary Care Center (pilot site, to be split into a new primary care clinic on the west side of Indianapolis and a primary care clinic in the new hospital outpatient building)
  • Continuity clinics
    • Wishard Health Services
    • Indianapolis Veterans Affairs Medical Center

Population Served

  • Health care professionals in Indiana serve one of the least healthy populations in the Nation, ranking 3rd in the prevalence of smoking, 9th in the prevalence of obesity, and 15th in the prevalence of cancer.
  • The thousands of patients served are predominantly females (60 percent) from low-income families of diverse cultures and ethnicities.
