Goals

  • Develop a curriculum in adherence with competency-based standards for accreditation that can be implemented as part of the HU Residency Programs within the first 4 months of the grant.
  • Implement the SBIRT Medical Residency Program curriculum as part of the HU core lecture series for all medical residents, and implement SBIRT training for residents in all primary care clinical settings.
  • Assess the effectiveness of the curriculum in increasing knowledge and changing attitudes and behavior toward SBIRT interventions.
  • Promote the adoption of SBIRT throughout the Washington metropolitan area through graduating trainees, symposia, and other programs for local, regional, and national medical communities.
  • Develop and implement a plan for followup to assess residents’ expertise in incorporating SBIRT into their practices.

Population Served

  • HU Hospital is located in Northwest Washington, DC, and serves low-income, diverse minority and ethnic groups and special populations that disproportionately suffer from a range of health problems, including heart disease, diabetes, chronic liver failure, high blood pressure, psychiatric illness, HIV/AIDS, environmental-related trauma injuries, and conditions related to substance abuse.
  • Patients rely heavily on utilizing the hospital as a resource for their primary health care needs.
  • Many patients are exposed to high-crime environments with illegal drug markets and a high density of liquor stores.
