Program Vision and Design

Strengths

  • The State views SBIRT as essential to behavioral health and primary care integration.
  • An integrated health information exchange (HIE) is part of the State’s vision.

Challenges

  • Behavioral health and primary care services have been historically separate.

Grantee Leadership

Strengths

  • Senior leadership strongly supports SBIRT.
  • Grantee staff are highly experienced.
  • The evaluation will support leadership decisionmaking.
  • The policy steering committee (PSC) includes essential stakeholders.

Challenges

  • The role of county governments in long-term SBIRT dissemination and adoption will require further definition.

Implementation Plan

Strengths

  • South Carolina SBIRT has the capacity to provide training in SBIRT and motivational interviewing at the practice sites.
  • South Carolina SBIRT effectively uses evaluation data to monitor and support implementation.
  • Nearly all primary care sites have electronic health records (EHRs) capable of participating in the State’s HIE.
  • The State and counties have collaborated to purchase a single EHR for use by all substance abuse treatment providers.
  • The State has most of the essential elements for an integrated data system for patient care.
  • South Carolina SBIRT is using a touch-screen convertible tablet to collect and report SBIRT information.

Challenges

  • Practice sites are in the early phases of implementation and are experiencing challenges with workflow, fidelity, and staff buy-in.
  • Primary care sites are just beginning to participate in the HIE.
  • The CareScope system does not have the capacity to exchange information with primary care, substance use services, or the HIE.
  • The substance abuse treatment providers are just starting to use an EHR. Their system has the capacity to exchange information with other systems but does not currently do so.

Community Linkages, Partners, and Participation

Strengths

  • South Carolina SBIRT has engaged key stakeholders in its PSC, including Medicaid and the State Office of Research and Statistics.
  • South Carolina SBIRT has good working relationships with the State’s primary care association and behavioral health provider association.

Challenges

  • None noted

Client Outreach, Recruitment, and Referral

Strengths

  • The project uses health educators to provide full screenings and brief interventions at the practice sites.
  • The project uses patient navigators to support clients referred to treatment.

Challenge

  • The grantee does not currently have capacity for brief treatment at health care sites.

Affordable Care Act Readiness

Strengths

  • EHRs are in use at most South Carolina SBIRT sites. SBIRT is being embedded in EHRs.
  • Sites will participate in the State’s HIE.
  • Essential stakeholders and policymakers, including Medicaid, participate on the PSC.

Challenge

  • There has been limited integration among primary care, substance abuse, and mental health services.

Sustainability Planning

Strengths

  • The PSC will play an important role in supporting long-term sustainability.
  • Medicaid and Medicare codes for screening and brief intervention are active in South Carolina.

Challenges

  • Medicaid codes limit the providers that can deliver SBIRT services and the circumstances under which services can be delivered. SBIRT is not part of the federally qualified health center (FQHC) bundled Medicaid rate.
  • Practice sites will need to develop viable business practice models for SBIRT within diverse practice settings. This is particularly true for FQHCs that operate using bundled rates for services.

Grantee Evaluation

Strengths

  • South Carolina SBIRT has an experienced evaluator.
  • South Carolina SBIRT uses a Web-based data reporting system.

Challenge

  • The project is not meeting Government Performance and Results Act screening targets.

Recommended Enhancements

GRANTEE-LED ENHANCEMENTS

  • Program Vision and Design
    • Collaborate with State and county authorities and communities to support behavioral health and primary care integration.
  • Grantee Leadership
    • Continue discussing the future role of county systems in disseminating and adopting SBIRT and in integrating behavioral health and primary care.
  • Implementation Plan
    • Consider adding a position to support practice site implementation with funding from the State primary care association, as other grantees have done successfully.
    • Develop a strategic plan to support HIT integration at the community, county, and State levels and guide allocation of resources. (The grantee has requested technical assistance and a plan template.)
  • Client Outreach, Recruitment, and Referral
    • Seek technical assistance for brief treatment implementation.
    • Have patient navigators assist with warm handoffs for treatment referrals when possible.
  • Affordable Care Act Readiness
    • Collaborate with State and county substance abuse and mental health systems to support behavioral health and primary care integration.
  • Sustainability Planning
    • Work with the PSC to pursue Medicaid changes regarding SBIRT and bundled rates.
    • Work with State primary care and behavioral health provider associations to develop viable business practice models.
  • Grantee Evaluation
    • Consider working closely with the project team and CareScope to address the issue of uncounted “screening negative” patients.
