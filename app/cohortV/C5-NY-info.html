Goals

  • Deliver SBIRT services to 150,000 New Yorkers affected by Hurricane Sandy.
  • Build technology infrastructure to ease provider burden and support sustainable SBIRT.
  • Marshal stakeholder support for policies that promote sustainable SBIRT.
  • Disseminate SBIRT programs to health care entities throughout the State.

Implementation Sites

  • North Shore-LIJ (the largest integrated health care organization in New York)
  • Four emergency departments in Nassau, Suffolk (Southside), Staten Island, and lower Manhattan
  • Seven primary care clinics, all adjacent to emergency departments, in Nassau, Suffolk, and Staten Island

Population Served

  • The 150,000 New Yorkers in the 4 areas impacted by Hurricane Sandy—Suffolk County, Nassau County, Staten Island, and lower Manhattan—are likely to experience a significant increase in substance-related problems and a shortage of services related to the disaster.
  • Staten Island has the highest rate of opioid overdoses in New York City and the highest prescription rates for hydrocodone and oxycodone. The area is highly diverse, with many working-class neighborhoods and communities of disenfranchised individuals, including significant concentrations of African Americans and Latinos.
  • The lower Manhattan neighborhood has many lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender residents—a population with high levels of risky substance use.
  • The south side of Long Island has a large Latino community (24 percent of the population) with many recent immigrants whose primary language is Spanish.
  • The north side of Long Island has a greater diversity of immigrant communities, with notable pockets of East and South Asian groups.
