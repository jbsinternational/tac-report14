Goals

  • Expand the use of SBIRT in hospital and primary health care settings.
  • Support clinically appropriate services for people at risk for or diagnosed with a substance use disorder.
  • Enhance and expand use of current technological strategies to embed SBIRT as a clinical and business practice.
  • Identify and implement systems and policy changes to increase access to treatment in generalist and specialist settings.
  • Expand the use of the Ohio Board of Pharmacy Prescription Management Program (or Ohio Automated Rx Reporting System) in conjunction with SBIRT to facilitate identification of potential prescription drug misuse.

Implementation Sites

  • Year 1: Three providers in four locations in the Dayton-West Central Regional Extension Center of OHIP
    • St. Rita’s Medical Center in Lima (Allen County)
    • Health Partners of Western Ohio in Lima (Allen County) and Kenton (Hardin County)
    • Family Health Care of Northwest Ohio in Van Wert (Van Wert County)
  • Years 2–5: Other areas of Ohio systematically included through the seven OHIP Regional Extension Centers
  • Years 2–5: Expanded to nongrantees

Population Served

  • The population of focus is adults who receive medical service in primary care and other community settings (health centers, hospitals, emergency departments, etc.).
  • The subpopulations served include the elderly, pregnant women, service members/veterans, and minority populations—particularly African Americans and Latinos, who often experience health disparities.
