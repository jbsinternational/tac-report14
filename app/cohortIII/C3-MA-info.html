Population Served

  • Boston University’s CASA CHESS project serves Latino/a adults (18 or older) in recovery from substance use who also have co-occurring mental health disorders and who have completed residential treatment at partner site Casa Esperanza (Hope House).

Project Goals

  • Staff will direct and oversee the adaptation of the University of Wisconsin’s Addiction Comprehensive Health Enhancement Support System (A-CHESS) mobile application (to be called CASA CHESS) to a Latino bilingual population and include a component that promotes medication adherence.
  • Staff will provide initial and ongoing training and support to clients enrolled in the project who complete residential treatment at Casa Esperanza.
  • Staff will provide intensive outpatient case management services to all clients participating in the project. The case manager will be available through the smartphone to respond to immediate requests from clients using the smartphone technology.
  • Peer recovery coaches in combination with the case-manager will be available to respond to requests from clients using the smartphone technology throughout this project period.
  • The local evaluation team will use a formative evaluation model, provide ongoing supervision, training, and technical assistance to Casa Esperanza staff, collect comparison group data and have ongoing weekly contact with Casa Esperanza staff.
